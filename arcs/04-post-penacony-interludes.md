# Arc: Post-Penacony Interludes (v2.3–2.7)

*For a detailed, beat-by-beat timeline, major events, and key revelations, see [`arcs/detail/04-post-penacony-interludes.md`](detail/04-post-penacony-interludes.md).*

## Summary

Between the resolution of the main Penacony story (v2.2) and the departure for Amphoreus (end of v2.7), the Astral Express crew works through a string of shorter, more self-contained interludes: Firefly's epilogue, an IPC financial-pressure story led by Jade, a Xianzhou Yaoqing side story introducing Feixiao, Lingsha, and Moze, a return to Penacony's dreamscape introducing Rappa, and a farewell chapter introducing Fugue before the crew sets course for Amphoreus. None of these stories involve the Stellaron Hunters acting as agents of an Aeon of Destruction; Elio and the Stellaron Hunters' arc here is tied to the Path of Finality, consistent with the rest of the compendium. The interludes function as a linked epilogue/wind-down rather than a new self-contained crisis.

After the main Penacony story resolves in v2.2, the Astral Express crew lingers on and around Penacony (with one excursion back to Xianzhou territory) through a string of shorter, more self-contained stories before finally departing for Amphoreus at the end of v2.7. This document groups these interludes together rather than treating each patch as a fully separate "arc," since they read more as a linked epilogue/wind-down than a new self-contained crisis.

## v2.3 — "And on the Eighth Day" (Firefly's epilogue)

Resolves the dangling thread of **[Firefly](../characters/detail/firefly.md)**'s fate from the main Penacony story. Firefly, a genetically engineered soldier from the fallen Republic of Glamoth operating under the Stellaron Hunter alias "Sam," suffers from **Entropy Loss Syndrome** — a degenerative condition causing her to gradually dissociate from physical existence, worsened by the destabilized Dreamscape. In the epilogue, the Trailblazer draws on the power of the Path of Finality to grant her a final wish (to see fireworks) before her disappearance. She is later confirmed by Silver Wolf to have survived and begun recovering, leaving the door open for a future reunion. This chapter also further develops **[Elio](../characters/detail/elio.md)**, the founder of the Stellaron Hunters, whose predictive "scripts" are revealed to have shaped much of Firefly's — and the wider cast's — path through Penacony.

## v2.4 — "Currency Wars" (Jade and the IPC)

A shorter story centered on **[Jade](../characters/detail/jade.md)**, a senior IPC Strategic Investment Department operative and one of the Ten Stonehearts, who arrives to press the IPC's financial claims over Penacony following the reconstruction loan extended at the end of the main arc. The story explores the IPC's methods of economic coercion as a softer, subtler counterpart to the more overt threats of earlier arcs.

## v2.5 — "Finest Duel Under the Pristine Blue" (Xianzhou Yaoqing / Skysplitter)

A side story that returns the crew briefly to Xianzhou territory, this time to **Yaoqing**, the majority-Foxian flagship, and its **Skysplitter** region. Centers on **[Feixiao](../characters/detail/feixiao.md)**, the Yaoqing's Arbiter-General ("Merlin's Claw General"), and introduces **[Lingsha](../characters/detail/lingsha.md)** and **Moze**.

## v2.6 — "Annals of Penacony's Mappou Age" (Paperfold University)

Returns focus to Penacony, this time to the **Paperfold University College**, where a corrupted pocket of the Dreamscape has taken root. The story's framing ("this dream world is like an old banana peel, covered in dark stains... extinguish this Mappou Age of the Dharma") continues the series' ongoing interrogation of Penacony's dream-based society even after the main Family/Sunday crisis has been resolved. Introduces **[Rappa](../characters/detail/rappa.md)**.

## v2.7 — "A New Venture on the Eighth Dawn" (Departure for Amphoreus)

A farewell chapter: the crew reunites with friends made across the Penacony arc for one last set of shared memories, and **[Sunday](../characters/detail/sunday.md)** — having stepped back from leading the Oak Family to live as an ordinary person — gives a final performance before the crew departs. **[Fugue](../characters/detail/fugue.md)** is also introduced here: the genuine **Tingyun**, whose identity had been stolen by the Lord Ravager **[Phantylia](../characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md)** back on the Xianzhou Luofu (see [`arcs/02-xianzhou-luofu.md`](02-xianzhou-luofu.md)), survived that ordeal and — after nearly dying in the climactic clash between Phantylia and the Trailblazer's party — was found and given a second chance at life by **Ruan Mei**, taking up the new name Fugue. In-universe material (including voice lines from Yukong, who knew the real Tingyun) reinforces this as the story's settled account rather than an open question; this compendium treats the Fugue-is-Tingyun connection as confirmed narrative fact, consistent with how it is documented in [`arcs/detail/02-xianzhou-luofu.md`](detail/02-xianzhou-luofu.md) and [`characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md`](../characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md). Black Swan's earlier suggestion that the crew investigate **Amphoreus** — a realm so isolated that even Akivili, the Aeon of the Trailblaze Path, could never reach it — comes to a head as navigator Himeko gathers intelligence and sets the Express's course for its next destination, beginning the v3.0 arc (outside this compendium's scope).
