# Arc: Planarcadia (v4.0–present, as of v4.4)

*For a detailed, beat-by-beat timeline, major events, and key revelations, see [`arcs/detail/07-planarcadia.md`](detail/07-planarcadia.md).*

**Status: ongoing / unresolved.** This is the game's current live story arc. Unlike every other arc in this compendium, it has not reached a settled conclusion, and details here may be revised or overturned by future patches.

## Summary

The Astral Express's sixth major destination: **Planarcadia**, a world connected to the Path of Elation and presided over by the Aeon **Aha**. The crew arrives to a celebrity's welcome — a sharp tonal contrast to the hostile reception Amphoreus gave them in v3.x — and discovers a theme-park-like realm built around spectacle, games, and audience attention, including recreations of locations from earlier arcs. It's later revealed the Express actually crash-landed on Planarcadia once before, off-screen, under very different circumstances than this visit.

## The Phantasmoon Games

The central structure of the arc is the **[Phantasmoon Games](../factions/detail/phantasmoon-games.md)**, a recurring, high-stakes celestial competition tracing back to eight "Mourning Actors" who once witnessed the Phantasmoon (a celestial phenomenon created by Aha) and performed shocking stories in hopes of gaining Aha's attention. In the modern Games:

- Eight **Supplicants** who survive preliminary trials each permanently bond with a soul-fused **Mask**, which reshapes itself to reflect and amplify the wearer's essence. There is no substituting, adding, or transferring Masks once bonded.
- Competitors accumulate **Wishpower** — essentially collective spectator attention — with creativity and spectacle rewarded above all else; there is little other formal rule structure.
- The prize, historically, has been a face-to-face audience with Aha and exactly **one minute wielding the full authority of an Aeon**. Past winners have used that minute for everything from small, humble wishes to declarations that reshaped entire star systems.

In v4.2 ("So Laughed the Masses"), the stakes reportedly escalate further: this iteration's prize is said to be elevated from a single minute of god-hood to **permanent Aeon-hood of Elation** for the winner, drawing intense factional attention — including reported objection from the **[Research Society of Happy Smiles](../factions/detail/research-society-happy-smiles.md)**. *Fact-check note: this specific plot beat (the prize escalation and the Research Society's objection) could not be confirmed against a primary source. The Phantasmoon Games wiki article's own "Change History" section — which already documents the v3.7 introduction and v4.0 rename of this event — does not list any v4.2 change, and the Research Society of Happy Smiles wiki article makes no mention of the Phantasmoon Games at all. Treat this detail as tentative pending verification against an official v4.2 story summary or patch notes.* If accurate, this puts the Trailblazer squarely in the crosshairs of every faction with a stake in who — or what — should hold that power going forward.

## Himeko's Hidden Past

Starting in v4.3, the arc pivots sharply toward navigator **[Himeko](../characters/detail/himeko.md)**'s backstory. **[Asat Pramad](../characters/detail/asat-pramad.md)**, believed to be Himeko's father, is unmasked as a **Lord Ravager of the Antimatter Legion and Emanator of Nanook**, the Aeon of Destruction, continuing the series' pattern (established since the v1.0 prologue) of Destruction's agents lurking behind seemingly unrelated crises. Asat Pramad confronts Himeko with a series of impossibilities in her official history: how she survived something called the **Weathering Curse**, how she won an earlier Phantasmoon Games, and how she single-handedly restored the long-abandoned Astral Express in the game's earliest backstory. His attempt to trigger the Weathering Curse against her fails outright — which he takes as proof that the Himeko traveling with the crew today is not, in some fundamental sense, the "original" Himeko at all.

## v4.4: "In Ravages Does the Whistle Sound"

The most recent patch escalates this confrontation into a full crisis. The Trailblazer must regroup Planarcadia allies scattered across the arc and race to rescue Himeko before Asat Pramad can fully unravel her secret, culminating in a confrontation staged inside a surreal painting-world as a force called **the Voracity** — a primordial hunger sealed within Planarcadia and unrelated to Nanook — awakens. Asat Pramad is defeated in this patch, but the full nature of Himeko's identity — genuine survivor, something that took her place, or something else entirely — remains only partially resolved as of this writing.

## Open threads

- The ultimate fate/nature of Himeko, and what "the Voracity" fully represents, are unresolved.
- The outcome of the Phantasmoon Games' raised stakes (permanent Aeon-hood of Elation) has not yet been shown, and the reported v4.2 prize-escalation/Research Society objection beat itself is unconfirmed against primary sources — see fact-check note above.
- Aha's own motives in orchestrating the Games at all remain largely opaque.
