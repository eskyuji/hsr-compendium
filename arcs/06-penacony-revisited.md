# Arc: Penacony Revisited (v3.8)

*For a detailed, beat-by-beat timeline, major events, and key revelations, see [`arcs/detail/06-penacony-revisited.md`](detail/06-penacony-revisited.md).*

## Summary

A shorter coda, set immediately after the Amphoreus arc concludes, that returns the Astral Express crew to Penacony — not to open a new crisis, but to fill in gaps the player (and the Trailblazer) never actually saw during the original v2.x story. The Trailblazer recovers memories the Stellaron Hunters had deliberately erased (via the Cremator Constance) to avoid drawing Nanook's attention before Amphoreus, and reconstructs the true account of Penacony through Elio's Script. The arc's headline revelation — that Stellarons actually originate from Xipe, Aeon of Harmony, not from Destruction — recontextualizes the series' recurring Stellaron-crisis premise, and the patch closes by teasing Planarcadia as the next destination.

## Plot

The Trailblazer learns that their own memories of the Penacony arc were deliberately altered: the Stellaron Hunters had a **Cremator** named **[Constance](../characters/detail/constance.md)** burn away certain recollections in order to keep events hidden from Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction. With Constance's help, the Trailblazer reconstructs the true, fuller account of what happened on Penacony by working through **Elio's Script** — the predictive narrative document that Stellaron Hunter founder **[Elio](../characters/detail/elio.md)** had been using to steer events all along.

This reconstruction fills in several character beats that happened off-screen or were only partially shown in v2.x:

- **[Robin](../characters/detail/robin.md)** is shown to have grown considerably more assertive and self-directed since the Penacony crisis ("a shiny new backbone").
- **[Firefly](../characters/detail/firefly.md)**'s connection to the **Iron Cavalry of Glamoth** and a mysterious threat called **the Swarm** is explored in much greater depth than the original arc allowed.
- **[Gopher Wood](../characters/detail/gopher-wood.md)** (the Dream Master / Watchmaker who conspired with Sunday) receives substantial new backstory clarifying his motivations.
- **[Black Swan](../characters/detail/black-swan.md)** is shown explicitly refusing to exploit the Cremators' memory-erasing powers for her own ends, reinforcing her integrity as a character.

## Major revelation

Contrary to what much of the story (and many players) had assumed, this arc reveals that **Stellarons originate from Xipe, the Aeon of Harmony — not from Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction**. This significantly recontextualizes the entire premise of the series' recurring "Stellaron crisis" structure established back in v1.x, suggesting Destruction's role has been more about exploiting existing crises than causing them outright.

## Setup for what follows

A radio broadcast late in this patch teases **Planarcadia**, described in-world as something like "a painting turned real" or a virtual space tied to Aha, as the Astral Express's next destination — beginning the v4.0 arc (outside this compendium's scope).
