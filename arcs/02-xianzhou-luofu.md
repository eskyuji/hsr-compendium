# Arc: The Xianzhou Luofu (v1.2–1.6)

*For a detailed, beat-by-beat timeline, major events, and key revelations, see [`arcs/detail/02-xianzhou-luofu.md`](detail/02-xianzhou-luofu.md).*

## Summary

The Trailblazer's second major destination and the longest arc of v1.x: the **Xianzhou Luofu**, one of six immortal starships of the **[Xianzhou Alliance](../factions/xianzhou-alliance.md)**. Kafka lures the Astral Express crew into a Stellaron-driven Mara-struck crisis engineered by the Disciples of Sanctus Medicus and the shapeshifting Lord Ravager Phantylia (posing as the merchant Tingyun), which forces Dan Heng to confront his buried identity as the exiled hero Dan Feng and tests General Jing Yuan's resolve against Mara corruption. The arc runs from v1.2 through v1.6, culminating just before the crew's departure for Penacony (v2.0).

## Setup

Kafka forcibly redirects the Astral Express to the Luofu, claiming her Stellaron Hunter colleague **[Blade](../characters/detail/blade.md)** requires rescue and that the Luofu suffered "a Stellaron Burst" recently. In truth, she is manipulating the crew into the middle of a much larger crisis. General **[Jing Yuan](../characters/detail/jing-yuan.md)**, the Luofu's Arbiter-General, enlists the crew's help — ostensibly to capture Kafka, but really because he suspects a deeper threat is unfolding aboard his ship.

## The Stellaron Crisis

It's revealed that a Stellaron was deliberately smuggled into the **Ambrosial Arbor**, a sacred tree at the heart of Xianzhou faith, by the **Disciples of Sanctus Medicus** — a fringe cult devoted to **Yaoshi**, the Aeon of Abundance. This corrupted the Arbor and caused it to grow unnaturally, releasing a hallucinogenic, corrupting "smoke" from the Alchemy Commission's **Elixir Crucible** that turns exposed people and Cloud Knights into violent, corrupted beings known as the **Mara-struck**. Long-lived Xianzhou natives are especially vulnerable, since Mara feeds on accumulated negative memory and regret.

## Major Revelations

- **[Tingyun](../characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md)**, a seemingly friendly and helpful merchant who accompanies the Trailblazer for much of the arc, is revealed to have been possessed by **Phantylia**, one of the seven **[Lord Ravagers](../factions/detail/lord-ravagers.md)** who serve Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction — a heliobus of the ancient Shapeless species who intercepted and took over Tingyun's body. The real Tingyun miraculously survived the possession (and later resurfaces as her own person, eventually becoming playable in her own right as **[Fugue](../characters/detail/fugue.md)** in v2.7 — outside the scope of this v1.x compendium). Phantylia has been sabotaging the Luofu from within the entire time as part of a conspiracy with the Disciples of Sanctus Medicus, and ultimately sheds the Tingyun guise to attack the party directly in her true, monstrous form.
- **[Dan Heng](../characters/detail/dan-heng.md)**, the quiet Astral Express archivist, is revealed to carry a deeply buried identity: he was once (or is a reincarnation/vessel of) **Dan Feng**, an ancient and legendary Xianzhou hero known as the **Imbibitor Lunae**, exiled long ago for reasons tied to his own uncontrollable power. Under extreme duress he transforms into this true, draconic form to fight on equal footing with Phantylia.
- General **Jing Yuan**, threatened with being overtaken by Mara/Antimatter corruption himself, asks Dan Heng to strike him down rather than let him become a danger to his own people — a moment resolved without his death, but which underscores the stakes of the Mara affliction.

## Climax and Resolution

The party tracks the Arbor's corrupted roots to a hidden underwater city, removes three protective Vidyadhara Seals, and confronts Phantylia in her true, monstrous forms. She is ultimately defeated and banished, but not before delivering an ominous warning: "the Destruction of the Xianzhou is nigh" — foreshadowing future conflict with Nanook's forces. A small funeral is held to honor the "Tingyun" persona the crew had come to consider a friend, though the real Tingyun is later confirmed to have survived.

Later content in this arc (v1.4–1.6) deals with the aftermath: escaped **Heliobi** spirits from a damaged Artisanship Commission furnace trigger a series of ghost-hunting side stories involving characters like **Huohuo** and **[Sushang](../characters/detail/sushang.md)**, and the crew spends time with Luofu institutions (the Divination Commission, Sky-Faring Commission, and others) before General Jing Yuan gifts the Astral Express a Cloud Knight summoning relic as a parting gesture of trust.

## Departure

By the end of v1.6, the Luofu crisis is resolved and the Astral Express prepares to leave for a new destination — the dream-world resort planet **Penacony** — which begins the v2.0 arc (outside the scope of this compendium).
