# Arc: Penacony (v2.0–2.2)

*For a detailed, beat-by-beat timeline, major events, and key revelations, see [`arcs/detail/03-penacony.md`](detail/03-penacony.md).*

## Summary

This entry covers HSR v2.0–2.2, the Astral Express's visit to Penacony, the "Planet of Festivities," where the crew uncovers that the resort's Dreamscape is built atop a Stellaron and a buried history of prison-camp trauma. The central conflict is Sunday's plot (with the current Watchmaker, Gopher Wood) to seize the Dreamscape and impose a suffering-free but choice-free reality under the guise of honoring the Aeon Ena, ultimately aiming for a world without Aeons or Paths at all. The arc resolves with Sunday's defeat and arrest by the Family, Gopher Wood's suicide, and an IPC reconstruction loan that leaves the corporation with lasting leverage over Penacony's future.

The Astral Express's third major destination: **Penacony**, "the Planet of Festivities" — a luxury resort world where visitors check into the grand hotel-ship **The Reverie** and experience a shared, curated Dreamscape while their bodies rest. The crew arrives to investigate missing Nameless (travelers like themselves) and finds itself pulled into the planet's defining secret: nearly everything about Penacony's happiness is a controlled illusion built on top of a Stellaron.

## Background

Penacony was originally an Interastral Peace Corporation prison/labor camp where inmates mined **Memory Bubbles** (vessels for a person's memories and consciousness). During a Stellaron-triggered uprising, prisoners rebelled against IPC control, aided by early Trailblazers **Mikhail**, **Tiernan**, and **Razalina**. The Aeon of Harmony, **Xipe**, descended on Penacony by invitation and integrated the surviving former prisoners into **[the Family](../factions/penacony-family.md)** — a pre-existing, universe-spanning Harmony organization that had already grown to encompass countless worlds, rather than a new governing body created specifically for Penacony. Mikhail — later known as **the Watchmaker** — partnered with the Family to transform the planet into a resort, using the Stellaron not to suppress the site's trauma but to build the Dreamscape directly on top of it, at a real cost to the population's physical and mental wellbeing.

## Plot

The crew is invited to Penacony's **Charmony Festival**, ostensibly to investigate "the Watchmaker's Legacy." Early on, the singer **[Robin](../characters/detail/robin.md)** and the Stellaron Hunter **[Firefly](../characters/detail/firefly.md)** both appear to be murdered within the Dreamscape, triggering an investigation that pulls in a widening cast: the IPC's **[Aventurine](../characters/detail/aventurine.md)**, the Nihility-aligned **[Acheron](../characters/detail/acheron.md)**, and the mysterious guide **[Black Swan](../characters/detail/black-swan.md)**, a Memokeeper who understands the Dreamscape's true layered structure.

The investigation reveals that the visible "Golden Hour" Dreamscape sits atop a much older, more chaotic layer — the **Primal Dreamscape** — and that Penacony itself is essentially "an island with high walls" sinking deeper into Memoria, isolating it from the rest of reality. A bellboy named **[Misha](../characters/detail/misha.md)** is revealed to be a dream-manifestation of the long-dormant Mikhail/Watchmaker; before dying he passes on his hat and pocketwatch, entrusting the crew with unfinished business.

## Sunday's Scheme

**[Sunday](../characters/detail/sunday.md)**, the Family's public face for the festival, is revealed to be conspiring with the current Watchmaker, **[Gopher Wood](../characters/detail/gopher-wood.md)**, to seize control of the Dreamscape and remake it into a new dream world "free of suffering" — but also free of choice and free will. Sunday frames this as honoring the Aeon **Ena** (Order), but his true ambition goes further: a reality without Aeons or Paths at all. The crew's first apparent victory over Sunday — aided by allies like Jing Yuan and Dan Heng — turns out to be illusory: Sunday had already trapped everyone inside a nested dream of his own making.

## Climax and Resolution

After breaking free and confronting the real Sunday, the crew and their allies defeat him; he is arrested by the Family. Gopher Wood, faced with the consequences of his role, takes his own life rather than answer for it. In the aftermath, the IPC extends the Family a large reconstruction loan — a pragmatic, self-interested move that positions the corporation for future influence over Penacony rather than a clean resolution. The Astral Express crew holds a quiet memorial for the fallen Trailblazers (Mikhail, Tiernan, Razalina) who started this chain of events generations ago, before turning to what remains of their own unfinished business on the planet.

## Note on scope

The "murders" of Robin and Firefly early in the arc are revelations/illusions within the Dreamscape rather than straightforward deaths — both characters' true fates are threaded through this arc and its v2.3 epilogue (see [`arcs/04-post-penacony-interludes.md`](04-post-penacony-interludes.md)).
