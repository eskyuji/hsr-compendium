# Herta Space Station — Detailed Plot, Timeline & Revelations

## Summary

This entry covers the v1.0 prologue at Herta Space Station: Stellaron Hunters Kafka and Silver Wolf use an Antimatter Legion assault as cover to implant a Stellaron in the Trailblazer, who is then drawn into a brief vision-confrontation with Nanook (Aeon of Destruction) during a Doomsday Beast attack before Welt Yang intervenes and seals the Stellaron's power. The Trailblazer subsequently chooses to leave with the Astral Express crew. The main correction made was to the internal event ordering: Kafka's cryptic parting message and departure happen immediately after the Stellaron implantation, not after the later Doomsday Beast fight and Nanook encounter as the previous draft implied.

Official Trailblaze Mission chapter title: **"Today Is Yesterday's Tomorrow"** (v1.0). See [`arcs/00-herta-space-station.md`](../00-herta-space-station.md) for the standard summary; this document adds a beat-by-beat timeline and an explicit list of revelations and turning points.

## Timeline of events

1. **Antimatter Legion assault begins.** Herta Space Station comes under attack while Stellaron Hunters **[Kafka](../../characters/detail/kafka.md)** and **[Silver Wolf](../../characters/detail/silver-wolf.md)** covertly infiltrate the facility under cover of the chaos.
2. **Stellaron implantation.** Kafka implants a Stellaron directly into the [Trailblazer](../../characters/detail/trailblazer.md)'s body. The Trailblazer is caught in the resulting energy discharge.
3. **Kafka delivers her parting message** to the newly Stellaron-bonded Trailblazer almost immediately after the implantation: that "there will come a time when you have to make an important choice," urging them to make "the right choice" so "all the confusion will come to an end." She and Silver Wolf then depart, their actual objective (whatever it fully is) apparently accomplished — this happens before the station's later battle with the Doomsday Beast, not after it.
4. **Astral Express crew mobilizes.** **[Himeko](../../characters/detail/himeko.md)** and **[Dan Heng](../../characters/detail/dan-heng.md)** move to secure the station and its passengers; station staff — Lead Researcher Asta, security officer Arlan — coordinate the station's defense against the Legion incursion. The Trailblazer, now bonded to the Stellaron, fights alongside **[March 7th](../../characters/detail/march-7th.md)** and Dan Heng through the station.
5. **The Doomsday Beast attacks.** A wave of Antimatter Legion forces and a massive Doomsday Beast assault the station. **The Trailblazer shields March 7th** from a lethal blast during the fight, an act that draws the direct, momentary attention of **Nanook**, the Aeon of Destruction, in a brief and terrifying vision-confrontation.
6. **[Welt Yang](../../characters/detail/welt-yang.md) intervenes**, pulling the Trailblazer back from the confrontation with Nanook — sealing/suppressing the Stellaron's power before it spirals out of control — and saving their life.
7. **The Legion is repelled** and the station stabilizes, with **[Herta](../../characters/detail/herta.md)** (via her usual proxy) taking a detached, curious interest in the Trailblazer's new Stellaron bond rather than treating it purely as a crisis to be managed.
8. **The choice.** The Trailblazer is offered the option to remain safely on the station or join the [Astral Express](../../factions/astral-express.md) and go looking for answers. They choose to leave, formally joining March 7th, Dan Heng, Himeko, and Welt Yang as the crew departs the station.

## Major events

- First (and, within this arc, only) direct encounter with an Aeon: Nanook.
- The Trailblazer's permanent bonding with a Stellaron — the inciting event of the entire series.
- Formation of the core Astral Express crew as it will exist through most of v1.x.

## Key revelations

- The Trailblazer carries a Stellaron and is now a person of interest to multiple factions (Stellaron Hunters, Aeons, and — implicitly — anyone else tracking Stellaron activity).
- Kafka and Silver Wolf's actions here are explicitly premeditated, not opportunistic — Kafka's cryptic warning implies a plan extending well beyond this single heist.
- Welt Yang's ability to intervene directly against a manifestation connected to Nanook hints that his own history involves far more than his mild "passenger" introduction suggests.
