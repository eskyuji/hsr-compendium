# Penacony Revisited — Detailed Plot, Timeline & Revelations

## Summary

This entry covers the v3.8 "Memories are the Prelude to Dreams" patch, in which the Trailblazer returns to Penacony to recover memories Kafka had the Cremator Constance erase in order to shield the party from Nanook's premature attention. The reconstructed "Elio's Script" fills in Robin, Firefly, Gopher Wood, and Black Swan's arcs, and delivers the patch's central cosmological reveal: Stellarons actually originate from Xipe, the Aeon of Harmony, not from Nanook as long assumed. The patch closes with a radio-broadcast teaser for Planarcadia, the Astral Express's next destination.

Covers v3.8, immediately following the conclusion of the Amphoreus arc. See [`arcs/06-penacony-revisited.md`](../06-penacony-revisited.md) for the standard summary; this document adds a fuller timeline and explicit revelations list.

## Timeline of events

1. **Return to Penacony.** Fresh off Amphoreus's resolution, the Trailblazer and crew find themselves back on Penacony — not for a new crisis, but to confront gaps in their own memory of the original v2.x arc.
2. **Constance's introduction.** A Cremator (a splinter faction of rogue and ex-Memokeepers from the [Garden of Recollection](../../factions/detail/garden-of-recollection.md), specializing in selective memory destruction) named **[Constance](../../characters/detail/constance.md)** reveals that she burned away portions of the Trailblazer's memories of the Penacony crisis, acting on **[Kafka](../../characters/detail/kafka.md)**'s direction.
3. **The reason for the erasure.** The memory removal was meant to keep certain events hidden from **Nanook's** attention — recasting Kafka's manipulations throughout the Penacony arc as at least partly protective rather than purely self-interested.
4. **Reconstructing "Elio's Script."** Working with Constance, the Trailblazer pieces together the fuller, true account of what happened during the Penacony crisis by reading through the predictive narrative document **[Elio](../../characters/detail/elio.md)** had used to steer the Stellaron Hunters' actions.
5. **Character fill-ins.** The reconstruction reveals previously unseen developments: **[Robin](../../characters/detail/robin.md)**'s growth into a more assertive, self-directed person; a deeper account of **[Firefly](../../characters/detail/firefly.md)**'s connection to the **Iron Cavalry** — a military force from her homeworld of Glamoth — and a threat called "the Swarm"; expanded backstory for **[Gopher Wood](../../characters/detail/gopher-wood.md)**'s motivations, including a reported (though not independently confirmable from accessible sources) fusion with Penacony's Dreamscape in an attempt to revive Ena; and confirmation that **[Black Swan](../../characters/detail/black-swan.md)** refused to exploit the Cremators' powers for her own benefit, reinforcing her integrity.
6. **The Stellaron origin reveal.** The arc's central cosmological twist: **Stellarons originate from Xipe, the Aeon of Harmony — not from Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction**, as the series had generally implied since the v1.0 prologue.
7. **Setup for Planarcadia.** A radio broadcast late in the patch teases **Planarcadia** — described in-world as something like "a painting turned real" or a virtual space tied to Aha — as the Astral Express's next destination.

## Major events

- A full recontextualization of the Penacony arc's events through previously hidden information.
- The most significant single cosmological retcon/clarification in the entire compendium: the true origin of Stellarons.
- Setup for the v4.x Planarcadia arc.

## Key revelations

- Kafka had the Trailblazer's memories of Penacony partially erased specifically to shield them from Nanook — a rare, concrete example of a Stellaron Hunter action serving a protective rather than purely self-interested purpose.
- Stellarons originate from Xipe (Harmony), not Nanook (Destruction) — meaning every "Stellaron crisis" across v1.x–v2.x (Herta Space Station, Jarilo-VI, Xianzhou Luofu, Penacony) was rooted in Harmony's influence, even though Destruction's agents (the Antimatter Legion, the Lord Ravagers) were the ones exploiting each crisis. Note: the Stellaron Hunters themselves are not agents of Nanook/Destruction — they operate under the Path of Finality per Elio's Script, working toward an eventual stand against Destruction.
- Black Swan's ethical restraint regarding Cremator powers reinforces that her enigmatic methods, however secretive, aren't without genuine principle.

## Open threads carried forward

- The full implications of Xipe's role in Stellaron creation for the wider setting's cosmology going forward.
- "The Swarm," introduced here in connection with Firefly's backstory and the Iron Cavalry of Glamoth, is not otherwise elaborated within the arcs covered by this compendium.
- The claimed Gopher Wood/Dreamscape fusion (an attempt to revive Ena) rests on a single secondary source and could not be cross-checked against primary wiki material; treat as tentative pending further verification.
