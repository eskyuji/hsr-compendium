# Planarcadia — Detailed Plot, Timeline & Revelations

## Summary

This entry tracks the ongoing Planarcadia arc (v4.0–4.4), where the Astral Express is welcomed by Aha, the Aeon of Elation, into a world built around the Phantasmoon Games — a soul-mask competition whose prize escalates from borrowed godhood to permanent Aeon-hood over the course of the arc. The central thread is the unmasking of Asat Pramad, a Lord Ravager of the Antimatter Legion and Emanator of Nanook (Destruction), who has spent years posing as Himeko's father to probe the impossibilities in her history — culminating in a v4.4 confrontation that awakens an entity called the Voracity and leaves Himeko transformed into a new identity, Himeko Nova. The arc remains unresolved as of this writing, with several major threads (the Voracity, Himeko Nova's implications, Asat Pramad's fate, and the outcome of the Games) still open.

**Status: ongoing / unresolved.** Covers v4.0–4.4 (the current patch as of this writing). Official Trailblaze Mission/patch titles identified so far: **"No Aha at Full Moon"** (v4.0), **"So Laughed the Masses"** (v4.2), and **"In Ravages Does the Whistle Sound"** (v4.4); v4.1 and v4.3 continue the arc without a confirmed public chapter title in available sources. See [`arcs/07-planarcadia.md`](../07-planarcadia.md) for the standard summary; this document adds a fuller timeline and explicit revelations list, and will need revision as the arc continues.

## Timeline of events

### v4.0 — "No Aha at Full Moon"

1. **Arrival.** The Astral Express reaches Planarcadia, presided over by **Aha**, the Aeon of Elation. The crew receives a celebrity's welcome — a sharp contrast to Amphoreus's hostility — and learns the crew actually crash-landed here once before, off-screen, under very different circumstances.
2. **Introduction of the Phantasmoon Games.** The central institution of the arc: eight Supplicants bond permanently with soul-fused Masks and compete for Wishpower (collective spectator attention), with the historical prize being one minute of borrowed Aeon authority.
3. New characters **[Yao Guang](../../characters/detail/yao-guang.md)** and **[Sparxie](../../characters/detail/sparxie.md)** are introduced, establishing Planarcadia's Elation-aligned cast and the Path of Elation's "Punchline" combat mechanic.

### v4.1

4. Continued exploration of Planarcadia and the Games; introduction of **[Ashveil](../../characters/detail/ashveil.md)**, later confirmed to be **La Mancha**, leader of the [Galaxy Rangers](../../factions/detail/galaxy-rangers.md) — connecting Planarcadia's cast directly to Boothill's and Rappa's earlier-established faction.

### v4.2 — "So Laughed the Masses"

5. **The stakes are raised.** This iteration's Phantasmoon Games prize is elevated from a single minute of borrowed god-hood to **permanent Aeon-hood of Elation** for the winner.
6. **Institutional pushback.** **Fulwish**, leader of the [Research Society of Happy Smiles](../../factions/detail/research-society-happy-smiles.md), publicly challenges the wisdom of this change, questioning what future it will bring to Planarcadia.
7. **Silver Wolf's expanded role.** She receives a major showcase of her reality/game-manipulation abilities, fitting naturally into a world built around games and spectacle.
8. Introduction of **[Evanescia](../../characters/detail/evanescia.md)**, tied into the Games' Wishpower-driven "Special Observer" systems.

### v4.3

9. **Asat Pramad unmasked.** The figure believed to be navigator [Himeko](../../characters/detail/himeko.md)'s father is revealed to be a **[Lord Ravager](../../factions/detail/lord-ravagers.md) of the Antimatter Legion**, an Emanator of Nanook.
10. **Himeko's history destabilized.** [Asat Pramad](../../characters/detail/asat-pramad.md) confronts Himeko with a series of impossibilities in her official history — her survival of the "Withering Curse," her earlier Phantasmoon Games victory, her single-handed restoration of the Astral Express.
11. **The failed curse.** Asat Pramad's attempt to trigger the Withering Curse against Himeko fails outright, which he interprets as proof that the Himeko traveling with the crew isn't, in some fundamental sense, the original Himeko.

### v4.4 — "In Ravages Does the Whistle Sound"

12. **The rescue.** The Trailblazer regroups Planarcadia allies scattered across the arc and races to save Himeko before Asat Pramad can fully unravel her secret.
13. **Confrontation inside a painting-world.** The climax is staged in a surreal, painting-like dimension as a force called **the Voracity** awakens.
14. **Asat Pramad is defeated** — though whether he's fully eliminated or merely set back is unclear as of this writing.
15. **"Himeko Nova."** A new Himeko identity/form surfaces in the aftermath, without full clarity yet on what it means for her ongoing identity.

## Major events

- Introduction of a new Aeon (Aha) taking a direct, hands-on role in mortal affairs, unlike most other Aeons referenced in this compendium.
- The escalation of the Phantasmoon Games' stakes to permanent Aeon-hood.
- The unmasking of Asat Pramad and the destabilization of Himeko's entire backstory — arguably the single biggest ongoing mystery affecting a founding Astral Express crew member since March 7th's amnesia was first introduced in v1.0.

## Key revelations (as of v4.4)

- Ashveil is confirmed to be La Mancha, leader of the Galaxy Rangers, tying Planarcadia's new cast directly to Boothill's and Rappa's established faction.
- Asat Pramad, a Lord Ravager, spent years posing as Himeko's father specifically to probe the impossibilities in her history.
- Himeko's survival of the Withering Curse, her Phantasmoon victory, and her restoration of the Astral Express may not be what they appear to be — the failed curse attempt in v4.3 suggests she isn't simply who she's always presented herself as.

## Open threads (unresolved as of this writing)

- The true nature of "the Voracity" and what its awakening in v4.4 actually means.
- The full implications of "Himeko Nova" for the character's ongoing identity and role in the crew.
- Whether Asat Pramad has been permanently defeated.
- The outcome of the Phantasmoon Games' raised stakes — who, if anyone, will win permanent Aeon-hood of Elation.
- Aha's own motives in orchestrating the Games at all.

This document should be revisited and revised once v4.5 and beyond resolve these threads.
