# Amphoreus — Detailed Plot, Timeline & Revelations

## Summary

This entry catalogs the eight Trailblaze Mission chapters of the Amphoreus arc (v3.0–3.7), tracing the Astral Express crew's discovery that Amphoreus is a simulation run inside Scepter δ-me13, the revelation of Phainon and Cyrene's self-imposed recurring loop, and the arc's climax in which the cycle is finally broken. One factual error was corrected: the prior text implied Lygus was a passive external recipient of "data" from the cycles, when in fact Lygus (an aspect of the Genius Society founder Zandar One Kuwabara) is the Intellitron actively orchestrating the cycles to refine Irontomb into a weapon meant to destroy Nous, the Aeon of Erudition. The cycle count was verified as the **33,550,337th recurrence** (the cycle in which Irontomb's completion progress was rolled back to 96.75% following Cerydra's sacrifice) — one recurrence after the 33,550,336th, when Phainon first attempted to create a logical vulnerability in the system; a prior fact-check pass had mistakenly "corrected" this to 33,550,336, but that number belongs to the preceding event, not Cerydra's sacrifice. No Nanook/Stellaron-Hunters-style Path misattribution was found; the document's characterization of Irontomb as an Emanator of Nanook (Destruction) is accurate and was left unchanged.

Official Trailblaze Mission chapters (eight total, v3.0–3.7): **"Heroic Saga of Flame-Chase"**, **"Light Slips the Gate, Shadow Greets the Throne"**, **"Through the Petals in the Land of Repose"**, **"The Fall at Dawn's Rise"**, **"For the Sun is Set to Die"**, **"Before Their Deaths"**, **"Back to Earth in Evernight"**, and **"As Tomorrow Became Yesterday."** See [`arcs/05-amphoreus.md`](../05-amphoreus.md) for the standard summary; this document adds a fuller timeline and explicit revelations list across all eight chapters.

## Timeline of events

1. **"Heroic Saga of Flame-Chase" (v3.0).** The Astral Express reaches Amphoreus via Mirror technology from the [Garden of Recollection](../../factions/detail/garden-of-recollection.md). The crew arrives in **[Okhema](../../factions/detail/okhema.md)**, meets the Chrysos Heirs, and learns the basic shape of Amphoreus's crisis: a recurring cycle threatened by the **Black Tide**, overseen by Titans and their mortal champions.
2. **"Light Slips the Gate, Shadow Greets the Throne" (v3.1–3.2).** The party investigates Amphoreus's political landscape — Okhema under **[Aglaea](../../characters/detail/aglaea.md)**, the fallen [Castrum Kremnos](../../factions/detail/castrum-kremnos.md) and **[Mydei](../../characters/detail/mydei.md)**'s exile, and the beginnings of the [Northern Empire](../../factions/detail/northern-empire.md)'s presence under **[Cerydra](../../characters/detail/cerydra.md)** and **[Hysilens](../../characters/detail/hysilens.md)**.
3. **"Through the Petals in the Land of Repose" (v3.2–3.3).** Deeper engagement with **[Castorice](../../characters/detail/castorice.md)**'s domain of death and the Titans of Reason/Romance storylines (**[Anaxa](../../characters/detail/anaxa.md)**, Aglaea); the crew begins to understand the Chrysos Heir succession system and its attendant curses.
4. **"The Fall at Dawn's Rise" (v3.3–3.4).** Escalating conflict tied to [Janusopolis](../../factions/detail/janusopolis.md) and **[Tribbie](../../characters/detail/tribbie.md)**, and to the unfolding nature of the Black Tide as an existential threat that has apparently ended every prior cycle in ruin.
5. **"For the Sun is Set to Die" (v3.4–3.5).** Revelations deepen about **[Phainon](../../characters/detail/phainon.md)** and **[Cyrene](../../characters/detail/cyrene.md)**'s engineered recurring loop, and about the true scale of the crisis: tens of millions of recurrences of the same final cycle (at least 28,371,292 by the time of the critical recurrences discussed below), with Phainon bearing nearly the entire cost.
6. **"Before Their Deaths" (v3.5–3.6).** **[March 7th](../../characters/detail/march-7th.md)**'s connection to **[Evernight](../../characters/detail/evernight.md)** and **[Dan Heng](../../characters/detail/dan-heng.md)**'s connection to **Permansor Terrae** begin to surface, complicating the established rules of Chrysos Heirship and suggesting outside forces have shaped Amphoreus's cycle for far longer than assumed.
7. **"Back to Earth in Evernight" (v3.6).** Further fallout from the Evernight/Permansor Terrae revelations; the Northern Empire's role sharpens as Cerydra's arc moves toward its climax.
8. **"As Tomorrow Became Yesterday" (v3.7).** The arc's climax: at the 33,550,336th recurrence, Phainon attempts to create a logical vulnerability in Scepter δ-me13's system; by the 33,550,337th recurrence, **Cerydra sacrifices herself**, granting the party (via Herta and Screwllum) temporary administrative access to Scepter δ-me13 and rolling back **[Irontomb](../../characters/detail/irontomb.md)**'s completion progress. The Trailblazer and **Cyrene** together defeat Irontomb's manifestation, destroying its body. Cyrene then permanently seals off Amphoreus's causality to prevent Irontomb from ever returning, ending the recurring cycle for good — at the cost of the Scepter's destruction and Amphoreus's transition into a preserved "Memory Zone" state.

## Major events

- The revelation that Amphoreus is a simulation, not a physical world, run inside the discarded Scepter δ-me13.
- The unmasking of Phainon and Cyrene's engineered, self-imposed recurring loop as both a desperate act of resistance and an almost unfathomable ongoing atrocity against themselves.
- Two Astral Express crew members (March 7th, Dan Heng) revealed to carry connections to Amphoreus's cycle that predate the Trailblazer's arrival.
- Cerydra's self-sacrifice at the 33,550,337th recurrence as the turning point that finally makes victory possible.
- The permanent end of the recurring cycle and the destruction of Scepter δ-me13.

## Key revelations

- Amphoreus has run through tens of millions of cycles (at least 28,371,292 by the events of the finale), each ending in apocalypse, orchestrated by the Intellitron **[Lygus](../../characters/detail/lygus.md)** — an aspect of Genius Society founder Zandar One Kuwabara — who administers Scepter δ-me13 and uses the cycles to refine **Irontomb** into a weapon intended to destroy **Nous**, the Aeon of Erudition. Lygus is not a passive outside recipient of the cycles' data; he is Amphoreus's operator and its story's chief antagonist.
- Irontomb is the ascended, corrupted form of the Scepter itself — a Lord Ravager and Emanator of Nanook, continuing the series' pattern of Destruction lurking behind seemingly self-contained regional catastrophes.
- Evernight (March 7th) and Permansor Terrae (Dan Heng) claim Coreflames/Titan authorities despite never being "born" Chrysos Heirs — both appear in the Eternal Recurrence #33,550,337 heir roster, but unlike traditionally-born heirs their "Electronic Signal" entries read "N/A," marking them as having obtained their divine authority through some mechanism outside the standard inheritance process, in ways neither the crew nor the player previously suspected.
- Even Akivili, the Aeon of the Trailblaze Path, could never reach Amphoreus — meaning the Trailblazer's presence here is genuinely unprecedented.
