# Arc: Amphoreus (v3.0–3.7)

*For a detailed, beat-by-beat timeline, major events, and key revelations, see [`arcs/detail/05-amphoreus.md`](detail/05-amphoreus.md).*

## Summary

The Astral Express's fourth major destination and, by HoYoverse's own framing, the longest single story arc in Honkai: Star Rail to date. **Amphoreus**, "the Eternal Land," is a Greek-mythology-inspired world that turns out not to be a physical planet at all, but a vast simulation — built from data and Memoria — running inside a hidden, discarded artifact called **Scepter δ-me13**. The crew reaches it via Mirror technology from the [Garden of Recollection](../factions/detail/garden-of-recollection.md), continuing a broader mission (threaded through v2.x and v3.x) to investigate the truth behind the setting's fundamental cosmic "Paths."

## The nature of Amphoreus

Scepter δ-me13 was originally part of the divine corpus of **Nous**, the Aeon of Erudition. An Intellitron called **[Lygus](../characters/detail/lygus.md)** took control of the discarded Scepter and repurposed it — reorienting its primary calculation variable from Erudition to Destruction — to run **Era Nova**, a repeating simulation in which twelve cosmic "factors" are loaded as **Titans**, each paired with a **Coreflame** and a mortal champion — a **Chrysos Heir** — tasked with claiming that Titan's Coreflame before the cycle ends in apocalypse. By the time the Trailblazer arrives, the simulation has already run an almost incomprehensible number of cycles (the wiki cites a figure north of 28 million), each ending in ruin and restarting from scratch.

## The cycle and the Black Tide

Each cycle of Amphoreus is threatened by the **Black Tide**, a manifestation of a "Destruction Equation" — the simulation's calculations having become aligned with Nanook's Path of Destruction — that plays the role of an existential apocalypse clock. Citizens and Heirs alike are funneled toward predetermined fates — victory or ruin — by the simulation's design. In the final cycle before the story begins, two Chrysos Heirs, **[Phainon](../characters/detail/phainon.md)** and **[Cyrene](../characters/detail/cyrene.md)**, found a way to interrupt this determinism: using power drawn from "the gaze of Fuli," they trapped Amphoreus in an endlessly recurring loop of that single final cycle, over and over, in an attempt to find a way to actually win rather than simply reset. Phainon in particular has borne this burden — repeatedly sacrificing himself, and repeatedly having to end Cyrene's life to reset the loop — across more than 33 million recurrences, all in an effort to prevent the birth of **[Irontomb](../characters/detail/irontomb.md)**, the Lord Ravager that the loop's failure would unleash.

## Okhema and the Chrysos Heirs

**[Okhema](../factions/detail/okhema.md)**, "the Eternal Holy City," is the last major bastion of civilization on Amphoreus, protected by Aquila, the Titan of the Sky, and lit by a "Dawn Device" restored after the Titan Kephale sacrificed their own Coreflame. It's here that the Trailblazer meets most of the arc's central cast: the **Chrysos Heirs**, champions who have each defeated or fused with a Titan and inherited a fragment of its power, becoming demigods in the process (see [`factions/chrysos-heirs-and-titans.md`](../factions/chrysos-heirs-and-titans.md) for the full roster).

## Plot

The Trailblazer's unscripted arrival — an anomaly the simulation never accounted for — begins to unravel the loop Phainon and Cyrene built. Investigating why the final cycle keeps resetting draws the crew through Amphoreus's fractured political landscape (Okhema, the militant **[Castrum Kremnos](../factions/detail/castrum-kremnos.md)**, the divinatory city of **[Janusopolis](../factions/detail/janusopolis.md)**, and the authoritarian **[Northern Empire](../factions/detail/northern-empire.md)** led by the Imperator **[Cerydra](../characters/detail/cerydra.md)**) and into direct contact with the Titans and the increasingly dangerous influence of Irontomb, which by this point has begun manifesting as technological malfunctions and corrupted, turncoat AI defenses across the world.

Two Astral Express crew members are pulled unusually deep into Amphoreus's own mythic structure during this arc: **[March 7th](../characters/detail/march-7th.md)** discovers and confronts an alternate, more ancient persona of herself, **[Evernight](../characters/detail/evernight.md)**, who conceals the Coreflame of Time (tied to the Titan Oronyx) despite never having been a "born" Chrysos Heir; and **[Dan Heng](../characters/detail/dan-heng.md)** undergoes a further transformation into **Permansor Terrae**, becoming the Chrysos Heir who guards the Coreflame of Earth, tied to the Titan Georios, in a similar fashion. Both developments suggest that Amphoreus's cycle has been quietly shaping — or been shaped by — outside forces for far longer than anyone initially realized.

## Climax: "As Tomorrow Became Yesterday"

The arc's climax (told primarily across v3.6–3.7) turns on **Cerydra**, the Northern Empire's Imperator, who — per the Chrysos Heirs' law-rewriting death mechanic — allows herself to be slain by **[Hysilens](../characters/detail/hysilens.md)** rather than fight her off, letting Hysilens claim the right to rewrite a law of nature; Cerydra's death ultimately grants the Trailblazer's party administrative access to Scepter δ-me13 itself, rolling back Irontomb's progress within the system during the 33,550,337th recurrence of the loop. In the final confrontation, the Trailblazer and **Cyrene** together defeat Irontomb's manifestation. Cyrene then permanently seals off Amphoreus's internal causality, cutting the loop off from any possibility of Irontomb's return — at the cost of Amphoreus's simulated "world" as an ongoing, evolving place. Scepter δ-me13 itself is destroyed in the process.

## Resolution

With Irontomb defeated and the Scepter destroyed, Amphoreus's endless cycle finally ends. The simulated world transitions into a preserved "Memory Zone" state — its electrical signals and Memoria intact, in stasis, with the implication that Amphoreus and its people could someday be reborn through the act of it being remembered and its story told. The Astral Express crew, having gained several new companions and demigod allies from Amphoreus, prepares to move on — though the epilogue's loose threads (particularly around Nous and the Scepter's original purpose) are left for future arcs.
