# Interastral Peace Corporation (IPC)

## Summary

The IPC is the galaxy's dominant megacorporation, nominally devoted to the Aeon of Preservation (Qlipoth) but in practice run as a profit-maximizing power broker that treats Stellaron disasters as business opportunities. Its agents recur across nearly every arc as antagonists, rivals, or pragmatic allies — from Topaz's debt collection on Jarilo-VI and Aventurine's personal stake in Penacony, to Jade's post-Penacony financial pressure campaign and the Marketing Development Department's outright destruction of Boothill's homeworld. The faction is written less as a single villain than as a consistent, coldly rational incentive structure whose danger comes from its institutional logic rather than any one agent's malice.

## Overview

The largest economic entity in the known cosmos — an interstellar megacorporation that effectively controls trade and the credit system across thousands of worlds. It presents itself as a champion of free commerce, but functions as a monopolistic power broker, and its agents appear throughout v1.x–v4.x as a mix of antagonists, rivals, and occasional pragmatic allies.

## Stated purpose

Officially, the IPC serves **Qlipoth**, the "Amber Lord" associated with the Aeon of Preservation (see [`characters/detail/aeons-reference.md`](../characters/detail/aeons-reference.md)) — leadership claims the corporation's wealth ultimately serves this being, though Qlipoth itself appears largely indifferent to IPC activity. In practice the IPC behaves like any other profit-driven megacorp, expanding into new markets (sometimes coercively) and monetizing Stellaron-related disasters.

## Structure

Seven major departments (Building Material Logistics, Business Consolidation, Marketing Development, Strategic Investment, Talent Motivation, Technology, Traditional Project), ranked personnel from P13 to P50 (board members), each headed by senior executives.

## The Ten Stonehearts

The elite roster of the Strategic Investment Department, tasked with "liquidating" bad debts — often ones caused by Stellaron disasters — through whatever pragmatic means necessary:

- **Diamond** (P47) — department head.
- **[Jade](../characters/detail/jade.md)** (P46) — presses the IPC's financial claims on Penacony during the v2.4 "Currency Wars" story.
- **Obsidian**, **Opal** (P46) — senior specialists.
- **[Aventurine](../characters/detail/aventurine.md)** (P45) — pursues a personal agenda regarding Penacony rooted in his own history as an Avgin survivor.
- **Pearl** (P45) — specialist.
- **[Topaz](../characters/detail/topaz.md)** (P44), accompanied by Numby — negotiates Belobog's historical debts at the end of the Jarilo-VI arc, ultimately backing off after Bronya reveals the ancient "Engine of Creation" and persuades her that Belobog's own resilience and technological strength make outside debt enforcement unnecessary.

## Recurring role across arcs

The IPC functions less as a single antagonist and more as a constant background pressure across nearly every arc in this compendium: Topaz's debt collection on Jarilo-VI (v1.x), Aventurine's involvement in the Penacony crisis and the reconstruction loan extended to the [Family](penacony-family.md) afterward (v2.x), Jade's follow-up economic maneuvering in the "Currency Wars" story (v2.4), and — most darkly — the Marketing Development Department's destruction of **[Boothill](../characters/detail/boothill.md)**'s entire homeworld under director Oswaldo Schneider, which stands as one of the clearest examples in the series of the corporation's pragmatic reputation curdling into outright atrocity when profit incentives are strong enough.

## Tone

The IPC is deliberately not written as a cartoonish evil empire — its representatives range from genuinely reasonable (Topaz) to coldly transactional (Jade) to capable of real horror (the Marketing Development Department under Schneider) — making it one of the series' more effective satirical targets: an institution whose danger comes from consistent incentive structures rather than any single villain's malice.
