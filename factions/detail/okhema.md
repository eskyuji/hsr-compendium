# Okhema (Detail)

## Summary

Okhema, "the Eternal Holy City," is the last stable bastion of civilization on Amphoreus and the primary hub for the Trailblazer's investigation, seat of the ruling Chrysos Heirs led by Aglaea. The city is protected by the Titan Aquila (Sky), whose Coreflame passed to Hyacine, and its Dawn Device was forged by Aquila and later reignited after the Worldbearing Titan Kephale sacrificed its own Coreflame to preserve the city's light. Compared to Amphoreus's other major settlements, Okhema stands out for its relative normalcy and functioning institutions despite the looming Black Tide and cyclical apocalypse.

## Overview

"The Eternal Holy City" and the last major bastion of civilization on Amphoreus — the arc's primary hub and the seat of most Chrysos Heir activity.

## Governance and protection

Led by the Chrysos Heir **[Aglaea](../../characters/detail/aglaea.md)**, a demigod who bears the Romance Coreflame (also styled "Weaver of Romance" / "the Goldweaver"), described as "a fierce politician who doesn't shy away from taking drastic steps." Protected by **Aquila**, the Titan of the Sky (whose Coreflame now belongs to **[Hyacine](../../characters/detail/hyacine.md)**, who guards it as a Chrysos Heir), and lit by the "Dawn Device" — originally forged by Aquila and later reignited after the Worldbearing Titan **Kephale** sacrificed their own Coreflame to save the city's light source amid the Black Tide's corruption.

## Notable locations

- **The Marmoreal Palace** — includes ceremonial "blessed baths."
- **Kephale Plaza** — a site dedicated to the Worldbearing Titan's miracles, commemorating Kephale's sacrifice directly.

## Role in the story

Okhema functions as Amphoreus's closest equivalent to a "normal," livable city amid an otherwise apocalyptic, cyclically-collapsing world — banquets, public life, and functioning institutions persist here even as the Black Tide and the recurring final cycle loom over everything. Its relative stability, contrasted against **[Castrum Kremnos](castrum-kremnos.md)**'s militarism, **[Janusopolis](janusopolis.md)**'s fatalistic divination culture, and the **[Northern Empire](northern-empire.md)**'s authoritarianism, makes Okhema the natural base of operations for the Trailblazer's investigation into Amphoreus's central mystery.
