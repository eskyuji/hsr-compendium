# The Cloud Knights (Detail)

## Summary

The Cloud Knights are the Xianzhou Alliance's standing military order, organized into per-flagship contingents (each led by an Arbiter-General) that exist chiefly to fight "Abominations of Abundance" and other threats across Alliance territory. During the Luofu Stellaron crisis, the weaponized Elixir Crucible's Mara-inducing smoke turned exposed Knights — including, in a climactic sequence, Jing Yuan himself — into a direct threat the Trailblazer's party had to fight through, exposing how vulnerable even the Alliance's most experienced defenders are to Mara corruption. The order includes prominent playable characters such as Jing Yuan, Yanqing, Qingque, Sushang, and Huohuo.

## Overview

The Xianzhou Alliance's military order, tasked with eliminating threats — historically "Abominations of Abundance" and similar dangers — across the Alliance's territory.

## Structure

Each of the six Xianzhou flagships maintains its own Cloud Knight contingent under an **Arbiter-General**, with an overall Marshal supervising all six. Known Arbiter-Generals include **[Jing Yuan](../../characters/detail/jing-yuan.md)** (Luofu), **[Feixiao](../../characters/detail/feixiao.md)** (Yaoqing), and **Huaiyan** (Zhuming).

## Members (Luofu contingent)

- **[Jing Yuan](../../characters/detail/jing-yuan.md)** — Arbiter-General, the Luofu's overall military commander.
- **[Yanqing](../../characters/detail/yanqing.md)** — youngest lieutenant in Cloud Knight history, Jing Yuan's protégé.
- **[Qingque](../../characters/detail/qingque.md)** — a nominally active Knight who treats the position with far less reverence than her peers.
- **[Sushang](../../characters/detail/sushang.md)** — an earnest recruit training toward full Knighthood.
- **Huohuo** — a Knight recruit drawn into the Heliobi ghost-hunting side content.

## Role in the Luofu arc crisis

The Cloud Knights become one of the Stellaron crisis's most direct casualties: smoke released from the weaponized Elixir Crucible converts exposed Knights into Mara-struck, turning the Alliance's own defenders into a threat the Trailblazer's party must fight through. Because Mara feeds on accumulated negative memory, the Xianzhou's oldest and most experienced Knights — otherwise the most formidable fighters — are especially vulnerable, a cruel irony the crisis exploits directly. General Jing Yuan's own near-corruption late in the arc, born of long-buried guilt and self-doubt, manifests as a trial the Trailblazer's party must confront directly, with Yanqing's resolve playing a key role in pulling him back — underscoring how close the Knights' own command structure comes to being lost entirely.
