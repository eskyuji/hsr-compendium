# The Phantasmoon Games (Detail)

## Summary

The Phantasmoon Games are the central institution of the Planarcadia arc (v4.x) — a recurring celestial competition overseen by Aha, the Aeon of Elation, in which eight Supplicants permanently bond with soul-fused Masks and compete for Wishpower (collective spectator attention) to win a share of divine authority. Historically the prize was one minute of borrowed Aeon power; as of v4.2 ("So Laughed the Masses"), the winner's prize has been controversially elevated to permanent Aeon-hood of Elation, drawing factional pushback (notably from the Research Society of Happy Smiles) and placing the Trailblazer at the center of the arc's central conflict.

## Overview

The central institution of the Planarcadia arc — a recurring, high-stakes celestial competition overseen by Aha, the Aeon of Elation, functioning as both entertainment and a genuine, world-altering mechanism for granting divine power to mortals.

## Origin

The Games trace back to eight "Mourning Actors" who once witnessed the **Phantasmoon**, a celestial phenomenon created by Aha, and performed shocking stories hoping to win the Aeon's attention — establishing the template every subsequent iteration of the Games has followed since.

## Rules and structure

Eight **Supplicants** who survive preliminary trials each permanently bond with a soul-fused **Mask**, which reshapes itself to reflect and amplify the wearer's essence; there is no substituting, adding, or transferring Masks once bonded. Competitors accumulate **Wishpower** — collective spectator attention — with creativity and spectacle rewarded above almost any other consideration; beyond the eight-Mask limit, there's very little other formal rule structure governing how a Supplicant may compete.

## The prize

Historically, the winner receives a face-to-face audience with Aha and exactly one minute wielding the full authority of an Aeon — power that past winners have used for everything from small, humble wishes to declarations that reshaped entire star systems. In v4.2 ("So Laughed the Masses"), the prize is elevated dramatically: this iteration's winner will receive **permanent** Aeon-hood of Elation rather than a single borrowed minute, a change that draws intense factional attention (including public objection from the **[Research Society of Happy Smiles](research-society-happy-smiles.md)**) and puts the Trailblazer squarely at the center of every faction's calculations.

## Role in the story

The Games structure nearly the entirety of the Planarcadia arc's plot, providing both the setting's central conflict (who should hold permanent Elation-hood, and what they'd do with it) and its narrative tone — spectacle and competition on the surface, with escalating darker stakes underneath, mirroring how Penacony's cheerful Dreamscape concealed real harm in v2.x.
