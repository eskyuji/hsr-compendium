# The Silvermane Guards (Detail)

## Summary

The Silvermane Guards are Belobog's surface military and police force, nominally under the Supreme Guardian's authority, whose Jarilo-VI storyline turns them into the setting for a conscience-vs-institution conflict once Supreme Guardian Cocolia is revealed to be compromised. Key figures Gepard, Serval, and Pela each embody a different response to that crisis, while Bronya's arc from enforcer to reformist Supreme Guardian resolves it. The faction is a purely mundane Belobog institution with no Aeon or Path allegiance of its own.

## Overview

Belobog's surface military and police force, nominally loyal to the Supreme Guardian — an institution whose better instincts (embodied by Gepard, Serval, and Pela) end up in direct conflict with its compromised leadership under [Cocolia](../../characters/detail/cocolia.md).

## Structure and role

The Silvermane Guards enforce order on Belobog's surface and defend the city against the Antimatter Legion and Fragmentum threats, answering ultimately to the Supreme Guardian's office. During the Jarilo-VI arc, the institution becomes a proxy battleground for the story's central conflict: whether loyalty to the Guardian's office should override personal conscience once that office is revealed to be corrupted.

## Notable members

- **[Gepard Landau](../../characters/detail/gepard.md)** — captain, and the clearest embodiment of the Guards' ideal, honorable self-image; ultimately sides with the Trailblazer against Cocolia.
- **[Serval](../../characters/detail/serval.md)** — Gepard's sister, a former Guard who left the organization (was "mysteriously fired" by Cocolia) and now treats her departure as a liberation rather than a grievance.
- **[Pela](../../characters/detail/pela.md)** — an intelligence officer who provides the Trailblazer's crew with crucial information during the investigation into Cocolia's manipulation.
- **[Bronya](../../characters/detail/bronya.md)** — enforces Guard/Guardian policy for most of the early arc before her conscience wins out; becomes the new Supreme Guardian by the arc's end.

## Thematic role

The Guards' arc-long crisis of conscience — do you serve the office, or the person you believed the office represented? — makes them one of the clearest institutional case studies in the series for how an organization can retain its stated ideals even as its actual leadership fails them.
