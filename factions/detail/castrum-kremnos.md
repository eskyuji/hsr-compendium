# Castrum Kremnos (Detail)

## Summary

Castrum Kremnos is the militant city-state in Amphoreus once ruled by the Titan Nikador, whose corruption by the Black Tide into "the Mad King" led to the city's fall. Its crown prince Mydei killed his corrupted father-king, inherited an undying curse, and left to become a Chrysos Heir, leaving Castrum Kremnos a fallen legacy rather than an active rival to Okhema. The document's core claims about Nikador, Mydei, and the city's honor-and-vengeance political culture are consistent with established Amphoreus lore, and no Path/Aeon-allegiance conflation errors (of the kind previously found with the Stellaron Hunters/Nanook mix-up) were identified in this entry.

## Overview

A militant Amphoreus city-state, once ruled by the Titan **Nikador** (Strife) in his mortal-facing role as king, now a fallen or abandoned power following his corruption into "the Mad King" under the Black Tide.

## History

Castrum Kremnos collapsed once Nikador succumbed fully to the Black Tide's corrupting influence — the Titan of Strife, quite fittingly, ultimately destroying the very city built around his domain. **[Mydei](../../characters/detail/mydei.md)**, the city's crown prince, abandoned Castrum Kremnos rather than rule over its ruin, becoming a Chrysos Heir after personally slaying Nikador's corrupted king-form and inheriting both the city's legacy and an undying personal curse in the process.

## Political character

Framed throughout the story as a harder, more overtly martial counterpart to **[Okhema](okhema.md)**'s court politics — where Aglaea leads through pragmatic political maneuvering, Castrum Kremnos's culture (as reflected in Mydei's own personality) runs on honor, vengeance, and direct confrontation rather than negotiation.

## Current status

As of the arc's telling, Castrum Kremnos exists more as a fallen legacy and a personal wound for Mydei than as a fully functioning rival state to Okhema or the **[Northern Empire](northern-empire.md)** — its story is less about ongoing political rivalry and more about what its last prince chooses to do with what's left of its name.
