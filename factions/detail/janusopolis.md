# Janusopolis (Detail)

## Summary

Janusopolis is the Amphoreus city tied to Janus, Titan of Passage, whose divinatory culture of fate and possible futures is now overseen by Chrysos Heir Tribbie, one of the "Holy Maidens of Janusopolis." Tribbie's authority stems from having stolen Passage's Coreflame rather than from a confirmed outright defeat of Janus in combat, a nuance the previous draft glossed over. The city's thematic focus on reading and negotiating fate makes it a natural, if underexplored, counterpart to the central mystery of Amphoreus's recurring final cycle.

## Overview

An Amphoreus city devoted to **Janus**, the Titan of Passage — a center of divination and the mapping of possible futures, now overseen by the Chrysos Heir **[Tribbie](../../characters/detail/tribbie.md)**, who claimed the city's Coreflame from Janus.

## Governance

Tribbie, referred to in-game as one of the "Holy Maidens of Janusopolis" (the compendium's earlier "Triplets of Fate" framing is unverified — see flags), holds a position that blends religious and civic authority as the city's steward. She is described as the Chrysos Heir who stole Passage's Coreflame, which is the source of her authority over Janusopolis rather than a confirmed battlefield defeat of Janus. The city's entire culture orients around fate, possibility, and the negotiation of destiny — a natural extension of Janus's original domain over paths and passage.

## Role in the story

Janusopolis's divinatory focus places it in a unique position relative to the Amphoreus arc's central mystery: the recurring final cycle and the question of whether fate on Amphoreus can genuinely be changed rather than merely repeated. The city's institutional expertise in reading and interpreting possible futures makes it a natural, if underexplored, thematic counterpart to the Trailblazer's own unscripted, cycle-breaking arrival.
