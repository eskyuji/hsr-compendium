# The Divination Commission (Detail)

## Summary

The Divination Commission's Luofu branch, led by Master Diviner Fu Xuan, provides intelligence and prophetic strategic support to the Xianzhou Alliance, blending jade-abacus technology with gifted individuals' prophetic abilities. Fu Xuan's own philosophy — that fate can be actively out-calculated rather than passively accepted — is often framed as a break from a more fatalistic tradition associated with the Xianzhou Yuque and its Master Diviner, Jingtian, though the specifics of her personal training history could not be confirmed from available sources. The entry's account of the Reignbow Arbiter incident is well-corroborated: it took place during the Third Abundance War via the Yuque's Cloudpeer Telescope (not a Luofu-specific crisis), and it resulted in real casualties, including Master Diviner Jingtian — the unverified claim that Jing Yuan personally authorized the gambit has been removed.

## Overview

Handles intelligence, strategy, and predictive analysis for the Xianzhou Alliance, using "jade abacus" technology and — in the case of gifted individuals — direct prophetic ability.

## Headquarters and leadership

The Commission's overall headquarters sit on the Xianzhou Yuque; on the Luofu, the branch is led by Master Diviner **[Fu Xuan](../../characters/detail/fu-xuan.md)**. Community accounts commonly describe her as having received her mystical "third eye" from a blind elder diviner as a child and as having trained on the Yuque before relocating to the Luofu, but these specific biographical details could not be independently confirmed via available wiki sources in this pass (the Fu Xuan character page returned repeated 402/403 fetch errors, and the Divination Commission page's coverage of her personal history is limited to noting she has been "summoned to the Yuque for questioning" as an adult, which does not by itself establish that she trained there as a disciple of Jingtian). This entry flags her connection to Jingtian and the Yuque's fatalistic tradition as plausible but unverified pending a working source; it should not be treated as confirmed without further corroboration, ideally from Fu Xuan's in-game character stories.

## Philosophy

The Commission's Luofu branch, under Fu Xuan, operates on a philosophy that fate is not fixed but can be actively out-calculated and altered through sufficient skill and willpower — commonly contrasted with a more resigned, deterministic approach associated with the Yuque's divination tradition. This tension between fatalism and active intervention is a recurring undercurrent in Fu Xuan's characterization and in the Commission's institutional culture more broadly, though the precise lineage of that philosophical split (e.g., a direct break from a teacher named Jingtian) remains unconfirmed by the sources checked so far.

## Notable action

During the Third Abundance War (Year 8072 of the Star Calendar), Master Diviner Jingtian — the only person with permission to operate the Xianzhou Yuque's Cloudpeer Telescope, the Alliance's foremost long-range divination and communication device — used it to summon the legendary **Reignbow Arbiter**, an unconventional, high-risk gambit undertaken as the Yaoqing and Fanghu fleets faced the Denizens of Abundance and the living planet Ketu Mirage, with the Luofu dispatching four Cloud Knight brigades as reinforcements. The Arbiter annihilated the enemy borisin fleet with its Lux Arrow, but the strike also caused heavy collateral destruction on the battlefield: nearly one-fifth of the Xianzhou Fanghu's personnel were destroyed, and Jingtian himself — Fu Xuan's purported former teacher — perished in the bombardment. (Earlier drafts of this entry described the event as a Luofu military crisis resolved by Jing Yuan's personal decision to trust the plan; that framing is not supported by available sourcing and has been removed.)
