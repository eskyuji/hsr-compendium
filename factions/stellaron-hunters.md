# The Stellaron Hunters

## Summary

The Stellaron Hunters are a small, elusive band founded and led by Elio, correctly tied to the Path of Finality (the Aeon Terminus) rather than to Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction — a common misconception the group's destructive-looking methods tend to invite. Per Elio's predictive "scripts," their true, long-game purpose is to position events and allies toward an eventual confrontation with Destruction, not to serve it. Members like Kafka, Silver Wolf, Blade, and Firefly are bound to the group not by shared ideology but by individual pacts and wishes with Elio, making the Hunters pragmatic rather than doctrinal. They are distinct from — and not allied with — the Lord Ravagers, who are Nanook's actual agents.

## Overview

A small, elusive band tied to the **Path of Finality** (the Aeon Terminus) who travel the universe seeding and exploiting Stellarons. They are the closest thing v1.x–v4.x has to a recurring antagonist faction, but — contrary to how they're often first read — they are not agents of Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction. Their actions are morally ambiguous and often genuinely destructive, but their underlying purpose, as directed by Elio, is to position the pieces needed to oppose Nanook, not to serve him. The group functions as something like a dark mirror to the [Astral Express](astral-express.md): both chase Stellarons across the galaxy, but by very different methods and, ultimately, toward compatible ends.

## Founding and leadership

Founded and led by **[Elio](../characters/detail/elio.md)**, "Destiny's Slave," who possesses the ability to foresee future possibilities and directs the group's operations through predictive "scripts." Elio has foreseen the Trailblazer's eventual direct confrontation with Nanook and has spent the group's entire operating history quietly maneuvering resources, allies, and events — including manufacturing crises that look, on their surface, like straightforward destruction-mongering — into position for that confrontation. Elio recruited both **[Kafka](../characters/detail/kafka.md)** and **[Blade](../characters/detail/blade.md)** personally; notably, individual members are not ideologically committed to Elio's larger goal. Each has instead made a personal pact with him, trading their service for the fulfillment of an individual wish (Blade wants an end to his unkillable existence; Firefly sought hope against her degenerative illness). This makes the group pragmatic rather than doctrinal in its loyalty — a band of self-interested performers in a script only Elio can fully read.

## Known members

- **[Kafka](../characters/detail/kafka.md)** — an elegant, calculating, and unnervingly prescient member who appears to be working from a plan only she fully understands. She personally implants the Trailblazer's Stellaron in the prologue and later manipulates the Astral Express into traveling to the Xianzhou Luofu.
- **[Silver Wolf](../characters/detail/silver-wolf.md)** — a teenage hacker with god-tier digital infiltration abilities, who treats reality itself somewhat like a video game to be cheated. She assists Kafka in the Herta Space Station heist and reappears periodically, with an expanded showcase of her abilities during the Planarcadia arc.
- **[Blade](../characters/detail/blade.md)** — a near-unkillable, self-destructive swordsman whose backstory is tied to the Xianzhou Luofu; his rescue (or supposed rescue) is Kafka's pretext for redirecting the Express to the Luofu in v1.2.
- **[Firefly (Sam)](../characters/detail/firefly.md)** — operates undercover within the group during the Penacony arc.
- **[Elio](../characters/detail/elio.md)** — founder and off-screen strategic architect.

## Relationship to the Lord Ravagers

The Stellaron Hunters are frequently mistaken for agents of Nanook because their operations repeatedly precede or overlap with crises the **[Lord Ravagers](detail/lord-ravagers.md)** — seven especially powerful agents who serve Nanook directly, including [Phantylia](../characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md) and [Irontomb](../characters/detail/irontomb.md) — are secretly orchestrating. The two groups are not allies: the Ravagers pursue destruction on Nanook's behalf, while the Stellaron Hunters, per Elio's Script, are working toward eventually opposing that same Path. The resemblance is largely a product of both groups converging on the same crisis points from opposite directions, which the story uses deliberately to keep the Hunters' true allegiance ambiguous for as long as possible.

## Role across the story

The Stellaron Hunters function as recurring instigators: Kafka and Silver Wolf kick off the Herta Space Station crisis directly, and Kafka's manipulation drags the Astral Express to the Xianzhou Luofu, where Phantylia — an actual agent of Nanook — is revealed as the true saboteur working independently of (and, in the bigger picture, in opposition to) the Hunters' own goals. Kafka has told the Trailblazer directly that the two of them are fated to eventually face Nanook, warning that it will be a "brutal struggle of Aeonic proportions" for which the Trailblazer will need all the help they can get — a warning that reinforces the group's true long-game orientation toward opposing Destruction rather than serving it. (A more specific claim sometimes circulated — that Kafka had an agent called a "Cremator," named [Constance](../characters/detail/constance.md), deliberately erase parts of the Trailblazer's Penacony memories specifically to shield them from Nanook's attention — could not be confirmed against wiki sources during fact-checking; no such character or event could be located, so it has been omitted here pending verification.) Stellarons themselves are understood to originate from Xipe, the Aeon of Harmony, not from Nanook — undercutting the assumption, encouraged by the story's early framing, that the Stellaron Hunters must be Destruction's agents simply because they deal in Stellarons. Despite their morally murky methods, the group's ultimate throughline is preparation for a confrontation with Destruction, not service to it — though the full shape of Elio's plan remains only partially revealed through the end of v4.x as covered by this compendium.
