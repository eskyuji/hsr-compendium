# The Family (Penacony)

## Summary

The Family is Penacony's ruling aristocracy, descended from IPC prisoners who threw off IPC control in a Stellaron-triggered uprising and were subsequently gathered by the Aeon of Harmony, Xipe, into a governing collective built around Harmony's ideals. By the main story it presides over a society whose engineered happiness carries real hidden costs, a tension that Sunday and Gopher Wood's conspiracy to resurrect Ena the Order and seize Harmony's power pushed to its extreme, cautionary conclusion. Following that conspiracy's collapse, the Family survives but emerges financially and politically weakened, with leadership of its most prominent lineage passing from the disgraced Sunday to his sister Robin.

## Overview

Penacony's ruling institution, born from crisis and, by the time of the main story, presiding over a society whose happiness is inseparable from a carefully managed illusion.

## Founding

Penacony began as an IPC prison/labor camp where inmates mined Memory Bubbles. A Stellaron-triggered uprising, aided by early Trailblazers Mikhail, Tiernan, and Razalina, broke IPC control of the planet. The Aeon of Harmony, **Xipe** (see [`characters/detail/aeons-reference.md`](../characters/detail/aeons-reference.md)), subsequently gathered the surviving rebels into a new governing body — the Family — as a chosen collective devoted to Harmony's ideals, transforming a liberation movement into Penacony's lasting aristocracy.

## Structure

- **The Oak Family** — the Family's most prominent lineage, responsible for its political leadership; led by **[Sunday](../characters/detail/sunday.md)** for most of the main arc, with leadership passing to his sister **[Robin](../characters/detail/robin.md)** after his arrest.
- **The Watchmaker's office** — a hereditary/legacy position tracing back to **Mikhail**, the original Trailblazer companion who partnered with the Family to build the Dreamscape; held by **[Gopher Wood](../characters/detail/gopher-wood.md)** during the present story.
- Household staff and lesser lineages, including figures like the bellboy **[Misha](../characters/detail/misha.md)** (himself a manifestation of Mikhail's lingering will), support the Reverie's day-to-day operation and the Charmony Festival specifically.

## Governing philosophy and its cost

The Family's core innovation — building Penacony's entire "Land of Dreams" identity atop an unresolved Stellaron rather than suppressing or removing it — produces genuine happiness for visitors and residents alike, but at a real, ongoing physical and mental cost that the institution has largely chosen not to reckon with directly. At the 2158 AE Charmony Festival, Sunday and Gopher Wood conspired to resurrect "Ena the Order" and seize the power of Harmony by controlling Dominicus with the souls of the entire Oak Family, an attempt whose effects rippled across the Asdana system before the Astral Express Crew — together with [Acheron](../characters/detail/acheron.md), [Black Swan](../characters/detail/black-swan.md), [Boothill](../characters/detail/boothill.md), [Firefly](../characters/detail/firefly.md), and Robin — thwarted it. The story frames this plot as an extreme, cautionary extrapolation of the Family's underlying philosophy: if a managed illusion is good, why not make the illusion total, and its architects divine?

## Aftermath

Following Sunday's arrest and Gopher Wood's death (which occurred under mysterious circumstances three hours before the festival began), the Family survives as an institution but becomes financially indebted to the IPC for reconstruction — a pragmatic bailout that also hands the IPC significant future leverage over Penacony's affairs, continued directly in the v2.4 "Currency Wars" story involving **[Jade](../characters/detail/jade.md)**. Robin, now head of the Oak Family, later negotiates Sunday's secret release from custody through a deal with Jade, underscoring how deeply IPC leverage now runs through the Family's affairs.
