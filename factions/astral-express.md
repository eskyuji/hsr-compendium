# The Astral Express

## Summary

The Astral Express is the found-family crew of the self-repairing train that serves as the player's home base, led by Himeko and later joined by the Trailblazer, March 7th, Dan Heng, Welt Yang, and Pom-Pom. Its stated mission is to retrace the path of Akivili, the Aeon of Trailblaze, sealing Stellarons and investigating worlds tied to that Path — a mission repeatedly derailed into local crisis-solving by the Trailblazer's Stellaron bond. Welt Yang's mysterious past ties to his former identity as the Herrscher of Reason and sovereign of Anti-Entropy, not to the Path of Destruction. Over the course of the story the crew steadily accumulates traveling companions from nearly every world visited, reinforcing the series' theme of chosen family.

## Overview

Not a political or military faction so much as a found family bound to a sentient, self-repairing train — the player's home base and the connective tissue of the entire series. See `characters/detail/` for individual crew members ([Trailblazer](../characters/detail/trailblazer.md), [March 7th](../characters/detail/march-7th.md), [Dan Heng](../characters/detail/dan-heng.md), [Himeko](../characters/detail/himeko.md), [Welt Yang](../characters/detail/welt-yang.md)); this entry covers the Express as an institution.

## Origin

Himeko discovered the Astral Express long abandoned and derelict, and personally repaired it into working order, beginning the crew's ongoing journey across the stars. The train itself functions almost as a character in its own right — self-maintaining, spacious beyond what its exterior would suggest, and capable of reaching worlds by traveling the star rails originally laid down by Akivili, the Aeon of Trailblaze, fueled by converting the crew's ongoing "Trailblaze" into a perpetual-motion-like energy supply — well beyond conventional travel.

## Stated mission

Officially, the crew searches for the Aeon of the Trailblaze Path — later identified as **Akivili** — and investigates worlds and phenomena connected to that Path. In practice, the crew is repeatedly drawn into local crises largely because of the Trailblazer's Stellaron bond and the machinations of the [Stellaron Hunters](stellaron-hunters.md), turning an open-ended journey of discovery into a recurring pattern of arriving just as each world's defining catastrophe comes to a head.

## Core Crew (as of v1.x)

- **[Trailblazer](../characters/detail/trailblazer.md)** — the player character; bonded with a Stellaron in the prologue and taken aboard by Himeko's crew shortly after.
- **[March 7th](../characters/detail/march-7th.md)** — found frozen in a block of Six-Phased Ice with no memory of her past; cheerful, photography-obsessed, and fiercely loyal to her found family.
- **[Dan Heng](../characters/detail/dan-heng.md)** — a reserved exile from the Xianzhou Luofu serving as the Express's archivist and guard; hides a much larger secret identity.
- **[Himeko](../characters/detail/himeko.md)** — the Express's navigator, who discovered and repaired the abandoned train and effectively leads the crew's day-to-day operations.
- **[Welt Yang](../characters/detail/welt-yang.md)** — a powerful, world-weary passenger whose mysterious past includes serving as the Herrscher of Reason and sovereign of the Anti-Entropy organization (from his prior appearances in *Honkai Impact 3rd*); acts as an older-brother figure and a formidable fighter aligned in combat with the Path of Nihility.
- **Pom-Pom** — a small conductor-mascot creature that appeared on the Express under unexplained circumstances; handles onboard administrative duties.

## Crew composition over time

The founding crew is joined by the Trailblazer in the v1.0 prologue and, from there, steadily accumulates traveling companions and allies from nearly every world visited — [Bronya](../characters/detail/bronya.md) and [Seele](../characters/detail/seele.md) from Jarilo-VI, [Sunday](../characters/detail/sunday.md) and [Fugue](../characters/detail/fugue.md) from Penacony, various Chrysos Heirs from Amphoreus, and so on.

## Thematic role

The Express's rotating cast of companions across arcs reinforces the series' central theme: family, in this story, is chosen and built through shared crisis rather than defined by blood or origin. Nearly every arc ends with the crew slightly larger, and slightly more emotionally entangled with the people and places they've left behind, than when it began.
