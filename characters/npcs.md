# Major NPCs and Antagonists (v1.x)

## Summary

The v1.x antagonist roster: Cocolia (Jarilo-VI), Phantylia/Tingyun (Xianzhou Luofu), and the Aeons Nanook, Qlipoth, Lan, and Yaoshi who shape the era's cosmology.

Names link to their full profile in `detail/` where one exists; Aeons link to the consolidated `detail/aeons-reference.md`.

## Herta Space Station

- **[Nanook](detail/aeons-reference.md)** — the Aeon of Destruction; appears briefly to the Trailblazer in a vision after the Stellaron implantation, establishing Destruction as a looming cosmic threat.

## Jarilo-VI / Belobog

- **[Cocolia Rand](detail/cocolia.md)** — the Supreme Guardian of Belobog and primary antagonist of the arc; secretly wields the planet's Stellaron and suppresses the truth about the Eternal Freeze to maintain order, ultimately at great human cost.
- **[Svarog](detail/svarog.md)** — an ancient guardian robot built to protect the mining district; initially hostile/skeptical, later allied after being convinced that unpredictable variables (i.e., people) have value.
- **[Qlipoth](detail/aeons-reference.md)** — the Aeon of Preservation; grants the Trailblazer a vision and new power during the climactic battle with Cocolia.

## Xianzhou Luofu

- **[Phantylia](detail/tingyun-phantylia.md)** — one of the seven Lord Ravagers serving Nanook, Aeon of Destruction; infiltrates the Luofu for the entire arc disguised as the merchant Tingyun, and is revealed as the true saboteur behind the Stellaron crisis.
- **[Dan Feng / Imbibitor Lunae](detail/dan-heng.md)** — a legendary Xianzhou hero from the distant past, exiled for reasons tied to his overwhelming power; Dan Heng carries his identity/legacy, which resurfaces at the arc's climax.
- **[Yaoshi](detail/aeons-reference.md)** — the Aeon of Abundance, worshipped (and whose gifts are twisted) by the cult responsible for smuggling the Stellaron into the Ambrosial Arbor.
- **Disciples of Sanctus Medicus** — a fringe cult devoted to Yaoshi, responsible for planting the Stellaron that corrupts the Ambrosial Arbor and triggers the Mara-struck crisis.
- **Baiheng** — Jing Yuan's late close companion from the Xianzhou's past, referenced in flashback/backstory content connected to Blade and the Luofu's history.

## Cross-arc

- **[Kafka](detail/kafka.md)** and **[Silver Wolf](detail/silver-wolf.md)** — see [`factions/stellaron-hunters.md`](../factions/stellaron-hunters.md); both recur as behind-the-scenes instigators across every arc in v1.x.
- **[Herta](detail/herta.md)** — while introduced as a playable character/station director, she also functions narratively as a recurring "observer" NPC whose true motives and nature remain deliberately obscure throughout v1.x.
