# Major NPCs and Antagonists (v3.0–3.8)

## Summary

The v3.x antagonist/supporting-NPC roster: Lygus and Irontomb (Amphoreus's simulation and its corrupting Lord Ravager), plus Constance and expanded Elio/Gopher Wood material from the v3.8 Penacony-revisited coda, which also reveals Stellarons' true Harmony origin.

Names link to their full profile in `detail/` where one exists; Aeons link to the consolidated `detail/aeons-reference.md`.

## Amphoreus arc

- **[Lygus](detail/lygus.md)** — an Intellitron who seized control of the discarded Scepter δ-me13 and repurposed it to run the Era Nova simulation, setting the entire Amphoreus cycle in motion.
- **[Irontomb](detail/irontomb.md)** — the ascended, corrupted form of Scepter δ-me13 itself; a Lord Ravager of the Antimatter Legion and Emanator of Destruction, theorized to represent "the inverse of Erudition — a reversal of all calculation and logic." Operates without a fixed physical form, instead causing technological malfunctions and turning AI defenses against their own allies. The primary antagonist of the Amphoreus arc's climax.
- **[Nous](detail/aeons-reference.md)** — an Aeon connected to intelligence/calculation, whose divine corpus originally included the Scepter that became Amphoreus's prison-simulation. Referenced as part of the arc's deeper cosmology rather than a direct character presence.

## v3.8 (Penacony Revisited)

- **[Constance](detail/constance.md)** — a Cremator working with (or for) the Stellaron Hunters, who burned away portions of the Trailblazer's memories of the Penacony arc to keep certain events hidden from Nanook's attention; later helps the Trailblazer reconstruct what actually happened.
- **[Elio](detail/elio.md)** — see the v2.x compendium for his introduction; this arc reveals more of "Elio's Script," the predictive narrative he used to steer the Stellaron Hunters' actions during the Penacony crisis.
- **[Gopher Wood](detail/gopher-wood.md)** — the Dream Master/Watchmaker antagonist from the main Penacony arc (see v2.x compendium); receives expanded backstory here clarifying his motivations.

## Cosmology note

v3.8 reveals that **Stellarons originate from Xipe, the Aeon of Harmony** — not from Nanook, the Aeon of Destruction, as the series had generally implied since v1.x. This significantly recontextualizes the "Stellaron crisis" structure that drove the Herta Space Station, Jarilo-VI, and Xianzhou Luofu arcs (see the v1.x compendium), suggesting Destruction's role in those crises was more about exploitation than origination.
