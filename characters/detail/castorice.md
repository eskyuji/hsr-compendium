# Castorice

## Summary

Castorice is one of Amphoreus's twelve Chrysos Heirs, having inherited the Coreflame and domain of Thanatos, Titan of Death, after defeating him in single combat. Despite wielding absolute power over mortality, she is characterized as one of the gentlest and most kind-hearted figures in the story, treating death as a mercy rather than a cruelty. She plays a central role in the arc's climax alongside the Trailblazer and Cyrene, contributing to the final confrontation with Irontomb and the sealing-off of Amphoreus's causality.

## Overview

Daughter of the river Styx and demigod of Death, Castorice wields absolute dominion over mortality — and is, despite this, one of the gentlest, most kind-hearted figures on Amphoreus.

## History

Castorice became a Chrysos Heir after defeating **Thanatos**, the Titan of Death, inheriting his Coreflame and a corresponding command over the boundary between life and death: her power can grant "the embrace of demise," making death itself something that arrives peacefully rather than violently at her hand. She's depicted with a skeletal, purple-aura dragon companion, and her visual motifs (a black spiked crown, a white dress patterned with purple flowers and butterflies) reinforce her role as death's gentle steward rather than its cruel agent.

She plays a central role in the arc's climax alongside the Trailblazer and **[Cyrene](cyrene.md)**, contributing directly to the final confrontation with **[Irontomb](irontomb.md)** and the effort to permanently close off Amphoreus's causality once the threat is resolved.

## Personality

Extraordinarily kind-hearted despite (or perhaps because of) the lethal nature of her power — Castorice's arc plays against the expectation that a demigod of Death would be cold or menacing, instead presenting death, in her hands, as an act of mercy and care.
