# Cocolia Rand

## Summary

Cocolia Rand is the Supreme Guardian of Belobog and primary antagonist of the Jarilo-VI arc — a ruler whose secret, decades-long concealment of the Stellaron beneath the Eternal Freeze, and her growing dependence on its power, curdle into an authoritarian regime that lets Underground Belobog's suffering go unchecked. She frames the Trailblazer's crew and exiles them to the Fragmentum, then confronts them at Everwinter Hill, where the Trailblazer receives a vision and blessing from Qlipoth, the Aeon of Preservation, to defeat her. Cocolia dies as the Stellaron's hold over her breaks, and Bronya chooses to let her be publicly remembered as a hero rather than expose the manipulation, to preserve Belobog's fragile unity during reconstruction.

## Overview

The Supreme Guardian of Belobog and the primary antagonist of the Jarilo-VI arc — a ruler whose authoritarian turn is driven less by malice than by a desperate, secret bargain made to save her city, at devastating cost to the people she was supposed to protect.

## History

As Supreme Guardian, Cocolia presided over Belobog's long isolation following the Eternal Freeze, publicly maintaining that the endless winter was simply the world's natural state. In truth, she had discovered — and suppressed — the fact that a Stellaron was the true cause of the Eternal Freeze, fearing the social upheaval that revealing this would cause. Over time she came to secretly rely on the Stellaron's own power to maintain her authority and Belobog's fragile stability, a dependency that gradually corrupted both her judgment and her rule.

This manifests as increasingly oppressive policies, particularly toward Underground Belobog, whose suffering she allows to continue rather than risk destabilizing the surface city's order. When the Trailblazer's crew gets close to the truth, she has them framed and driven into the Fragmentum, setting the arc's second act in motion. In the climactic confrontation at Everwinter Hill, her Stellaron-empowered assault nearly overwhelms the Trailblazer, who only turns the tide with a vision and blessing from Qlipoth, the Aeon of Preservation.

## Personality

Cocolia reads, until her unraveling, as a competent and even sympathetic ruler — someone who made one catastrophic, secret compromise years earlier and has been managing its consequences (and its psychological toll) ever since. Her tragedy is that her attempt to protect Belobog from panic and chaos is precisely what allows real harm to compound unchecked for years.

## Legacy

Rather than expose her manipulation and corruption publicly, **[Bronya](bronya.md)** and the Trailblazer's crew choose to let Cocolia be remembered as a hero who sacrificed herself for the city — a decision made to preserve Belobog's morale and unity during a fragile moment of reconstruction and reconciliation between the surface and the Underground.
