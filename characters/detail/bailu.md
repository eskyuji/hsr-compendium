# Bailu

## Summary

Bailu is a Vidyadhara physician-in-training on the Xianzhou Luofu whose innocent present-day life conceals a dark origin: she is the reincarnation of a monstrous dragon born from Dan Feng's forbidden attempt to resurrect Baiheng, a creature ultimately slain by Jingliu. The entry's core claims about her origin, her role as a doctor for the Alchemy Commission, and her supervised status among the Vidyadhara elders are consistent with established lore, and it does not repeat the Stellaron Hunters/Nanook (Destruction) misattribution found elsewhere in the compendium, since Bailu has no Aeon- or Path-allegiance claims that need correcting.

## Overview

A young Vidyadhara physician-in-training whose cheerful, rebellious personality sits atop an origin story tangled directly into the Xianzhou's deepest tragedies.

## History

Bailu was reincarnated from a draconic abomination that **Dan Feng** (the historical figure whose identity [Dan Heng](dan-heng.md) carries) created in a desperate, failed attempt to resurrect **Baiheng**. In that earlier form, she was killed by the legendary swordmaster **Jingliu** before being reborn into her current identity. This origin ties her, whether she's fully aware of it or not, directly into the same tragic history that defines Dan Heng, Jingliu, and [Jing Yuan](jing-yuan.md)'s shared past.

In her present life, Bailu works as a physician for the [Alchemy Commission](../../factions/detail/alchemy-and-artisanship-commissions.md) on the Xianzhou Luofu, known affectionately as the "Healer Lady" for her medical skill and her inability to stand by and watch people suffer. She remains closely monitored by the Preceptors (Vidyadhara elders) due to her powerful, potentially unstable abilities — she hasn't experienced the ancestral dreams typical of other high elders, and her tail is kept shackled to prevent her lightning- and water-commanding powers from slipping out of control.

## Personality

Warm and practical as a healer — she's just as likely to prescribe hydration and rest as anything more exotic — but with a playful, mildly rebellious streak underneath the professionalism; her private case notes reveal frustration with her strict guardians and a real yearning for freedom and adventure beyond her closely supervised life.

## Related figures

**Dan Feng/Dan Heng** (indirectly responsible for her origin), **Jingliu** (killed her previous draconic form), **Baiheng** (the figure Dan Feng was trying to resurrect when Bailu's original form was created).
