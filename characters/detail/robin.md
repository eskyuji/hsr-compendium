# Robin

## Summary

Robin is a celebrated Penacony singer and Sunday's younger sister, whose apparent murder at the Charmony Festival — actually a Masked Fool-engineered illusion, with Sparkle impersonating her at points during the festival — kicks off the Penacony arc's central investigation. Once Sunday's conspiracy against the Dreamscape collapses and he is arrested, leadership of the Oak Family passes to Robin. By the v3.8 Penacony-revisited coda she is shown to have grown considerably more assertive and self-directed since the original crisis.

## Overview

A celebrated singer performing at Penacony's Charmony Festival, and Sunday's younger sister — whose apparent murder early in the Penacony arc kicks off the story's central investigation.

## History

Robin grew up alongside **[Sunday](sunday.md)**, sharing a close sibling bond built around music; the two cared for a small bird together as children as a shared symbol of hope. As an adult, she becomes one of Penacony's most beloved performers, invited to headline the Charmony Festival. Early in the arc, she appears to be murdered within the Dreamscape — a figure found in a pool with a purple slash across her chest, dissolving before anyone can properly investigate — which turns out to be connected to **[Masked Fool](../../factions/masked-fools.md)** interference (Sparkle impersonates her at points during the festival) and the arc's wider web of illusions and misdirection.

Following Sunday's arrest for his scheme to seize the Dreamscape, leadership of the Oak Family passes from her disgraced brother to Robin herself, making her the Family's public head in the arc's aftermath.

By the v3.8 Penacony-revisited coda, it's revealed that Robin has grown considerably more assertive and self-directed in the time since the original crisis — described as gaining "a shiny new backbone" — suggesting real personal growth in the arc's aftermath that the main story didn't have room to show directly.

## Personality

Warm and beloved by her public, but defined in the story chiefly through her relationship to Sunday and the mystery surrounding her apparent death — a mystery that, once resolved, gives way to a more grounded, confident version of her.

## Faction

Performer for the Charmony Festival, and later head of the Oak Family (Penacony's ruling lineage) following Sunday's arrest. See [`factions/penacony-family.md`](../../factions/penacony-family.md).

## Related figures

**Sunday** (brother), **Sparkle** (impersonates her during the festival).
