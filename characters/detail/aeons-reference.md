# The Aeons (Reference)

## Summary

This entry catalogs the eleven Aeons named across the compendium's v1.x–v4.x arcs, from Nanook (Destruction) and Qlipoth (Preservation) through the newly-active Aha (Elation) in Planarcadia. The audit corrected a fabricated claim, repeated in two sections, that Stellarons "originate from Xipe (Harmony) rather than Nanook" — this contradicts consistently established lore that Stellarons are crystallized manifestations of Destruction, and echoes the same kind of Aeon-misattribution error previously fixed for the Stellaron Hunters elsewhere in the compendium. This document's existing framing of the Stellaron Hunters as adherents of Finality (not Destruction's agents) was already accurate and required no change. A minor consistency fix also names Nous explicitly as the Aeon of Erudition.

Aeons are god-like beings, each embodying an abstract concept ("Path"). Mortals who fully commit to an Aeon's Path can become that Aeon's Emanator, gaining a fraction of its power. This is a consolidated reference for the Aeons named across v1.x–v4.x, since most appear only briefly or through their effects on the story rather than as characters with an extended personal history.

## Nanook — Aeon of Destruction

First encountered in vision form during the v1.0 Herta Space Station prologue, when the newly Stellaron-marked Trailblazer briefly draws his attention. Nanook never appears as a conventional character; instead, his presence is felt entirely through his agents — the **[Lord Ravagers](../../factions/detail/lord-ravagers.md)** (including [Phantylia](tingyun-phantylia.md), [Irontomb](irontomb.md), and [Asat Pramad](asat-pramad.md)) and their Emanators — who recur across every arc in this compendium as the series' most consistent antagonistic force. Stellarons are established across the series as crystallized manifestations of Destruction itself — Herta and others describe them this way from the Jarilo-VI arc onward — so Nanook remains their true point of origin. The **[Stellaron Hunters](../../factions/stellaron-hunters.md)** ([Kafka](kafka.md), [Silver Wolf](silver-wolf.md), and their founder [Elio](elio.md)) are commonly mistaken for Destruction's agents because their operations repeatedly intersect with the Lord Ravagers' crises, but they are tied to the Path of Finality, not Nanook's.

## Qlipoth — Aeon of Preservation

Appears to the Trailblazer during the climactic battle with [Cocolia](cocolia.md) on Jarilo-VI (v1.x), granting a vision and new power (a flaming lance) that turns the tide of the fight. Also referenced as the "Amber Lord" the [Interastral Peace Corporation](../../factions/interastral-peace-corporation.md) claims to ultimately serve, though the IPC's actual behavior suggests this stated devotion is more corporate branding than genuine, active guidance from Qlipoth.

## Lan — Aeon of the Hunt

Patron deity of the **[Xianzhou Alliance](../../factions/xianzhou-alliance.md)**; according to Xianzhou lore, Lan destroyed a corrupted Ambrosial Arbor in the distant past and set the Xianzhou people on their founding mission to "patrol the world and eradicate all Abominations of Abundance."

## Yaoshi — Aeon of Abundance

Worshipped by the Disciples of Sanctus Medicus, the fringe cult responsible for smuggling a Stellaron into the Ambrosial Arbor and triggering the Mara-struck crisis during the Xianzhou Luofu arc (v1.x) — a case of an Aeon's gifts being twisted by followers rather than the Aeon acting directly.

## Xipe — Aeon of Harmony

Bound the survivors of Penacony's original IPC prison-camp uprising into **[the Family](../../factions/penacony-family.md)**, establishing the governing structure that would eventually build Penacony's Dreamscape (v2.x).

## Ena — Aeon of Order

The enigmatic Aeon invoked — and arguably distorted — as the philosophical justification behind [Sunday](sunday.md)'s scheme to remake Penacony's Dreamscape into a world free of suffering and free will (v2.x). Ena's own nature and motives remain deliberately unclear.

## Akivili — Aeon of the Trailblaze Path

Identified in v2.7 as the Aeon connected to the Trailblazer's own Path, notable chiefly for being unable to reach Amphoreus — a realm "so enigmatic that even the Aeon of Trailblaze... could not reach it," which is part of what makes Amphoreus (v3.x) such an unusual, isolated destination for the Astral Express.

## Terminus — Aeon of Finality

The Aeon corresponding to the Path of Finality, a Path defined thematically as standing opposed to predetermined destiny — its followers are described as "traveling on the Path of Finality but struggling against your destiny, attempting to move in the opposite direction." The **Stellaron Hunters**, led by [Elio](elio.md), are the compendium's clearest example of Finality's adherents: rather than serving Nanook, they operate — per Elio's predictive "scripts" — toward eventually opposing Destruction, even though their morally ambiguous methods and their proximity to Stellaron crises make them easy to mistake for Destruction's agents on a first read. See [`factions/stellaron-hunters.md`](../../factions/stellaron-hunters.md).

## Fuli — Aeon of Remembrance

Patron of the **[Garden of Recollection](../../factions/detail/garden-of-recollection.md)**, the faction [Black Swan](black-swan.md) belongs to (v2.x). Also the source of the power ("the gaze of Fuli") that [Phainon](phainon.md) and [Cyrene](cyrene.md) used to trap Amphoreus in its recurring final cycle (v3.x) — connecting Remembrance thematically to both memory-keeping and, more surprisingly, to breaking (or at least freezing) causality itself.

## Nous — Aeon of Erudition

Referenced in the Amphoreus arc (v3.x) as the Aeon whose divine corpus originally included Scepter δ-me13 — the artifact that, after being discarded and repurposed by the Intellitron [Lygus](lygus.md), became the simulation housing all of Amphoreus. Nous does not appear directly within the arcs covered by this compendium.

## Aha — Aeon of Elation

Presides directly and personally over Planarcadia (v4.x, ongoing), orchestrating the Phantasmoon Games and controlling their ultimate prize — up to and including, as of v4.2, permanent Aeon-hood of Elation itself for a winning Supplicant. Aha is unusual among the Aeons referenced in this compendium for taking such a direct, hands-on role in mortal affairs rather than acting primarily through intermediaries.

## Note

This list reflects only the Aeons directly named within the v1.x–v4.x arcs covered by this compendium. The wider Honkai: Star Rail cosmology includes additional Aeons and Paths not yet encountered in the story as told through these arcs.
