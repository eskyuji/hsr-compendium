# Misha

## Summary

Misha is a bellboy at Penacony's Reverie hotel-ship who turns out to be a dream-manifestation of Mikhail, one of the three early Trailblazers (with Tiernan and Razalina) who helped free Penacony's original IPC prisoners generations earlier. Before dying within the Dreamscape, he passes his hat and pocketwatch to the Astral Express crew, linking his brief arc to Penacony's founding history and the office of the current Watchmaker, Gopher Wood. No factual corrections were needed; this note adds a summary and slightly tightens the description of Mikhail's identity for consistency with the rest of the compendium.

## Overview

A bellboy at the Reverie hotel-ship, revealed to be a child-form dream-manifestation of the long-dormant Watchmaker, Mikhail — one of the Penacony arc's quieter, more melancholy reveals.

## History

Misha appears throughout the early Penacony arc as an ordinary, helpful bellboy, before being revealed as a manifestation created during Mikhail's prolonged, decades-long dream-state — effectively a childlike echo of the original Watchmaker's identity, existing within the Dreamscape he himself helped build. Before dying, Misha passes his hat and pocketwatch to the Astral Express crew, entrusting them with unfinished business tied to Penacony's founding generation.

## Significance

Misha's brief presence in the story is a direct bridge to Penacony's founding history — the uprising of the early Trailblazers Mikhail, Tiernan, and Razalina against the original IPC prison camp, and the [Family](../../factions/penacony-family.md)'s subsequent transformation of the planet into a resort built atop that unresolved trauma. His hat and pocketwatch become meaningful objects the crew carries forward through the rest of the arc.

## Related figures

**Mikhail/the first Watchmaker** (the identity Misha manifests from), **Tiernan** and **Razalina** (the other two early Trailblazers who fought alongside Mikhail in Penacony's founding uprising), **[Gopher Wood](gopher-wood.md)** (the current Watchmaker, whose office Misha's existence indirectly comments on).
