# Fugue

## Summary

Fugue is a survivor introduced in v2.7 who is given a second chance at life by Ruan Mei after nearly dying in a climactic clash with the Lord Ravager Phantylia on the Xianzhou Luofu. In-universe voice lines (notably from Yukong) tie her closely to the earlier Xianzhou merchant character Tingyun, whose real identity had been stolen by Phantylia — but this compendium treats the Fugue-is-Tingyun connection as strongly implied rather than a flatly confirmed fact, consistent with how the identity is handled elsewhere in the story. Her gameplay identity centers on breaking enemy toughness, framed narratively as a shift from indirection and disguise toward directness and reclaimed agency.

## Overview

The survivor identity introduced in v2.7, widely believed (via strong in-universe implication rather than an explicit confirmation) to be the real **[Tingyun](tingyun-phantylia.md)** given a second chance at life — a direct continuation of one of the Xianzhou Luofu arc's most dramatic character reveals, carried forward into the Penacony era.

## History

During the Xianzhou Luofu arc, the merchant "Tingyun" who traveled with the crew was revealed to be Phantylia, a Lord Ravager of the Antimatter Legion in service to Nanook, Aeon of Destruction, who had stolen the real Tingyun's identity after ambushing her ship. The real Tingyun survived that ordeal, unbeknownst to the crew at the time (see [`characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md`](tingyun-phantylia.md)). After nearly dying in the climactic clash between Phantylia and the Trailblazer's party, this survivor is found and saved by **Ruan Mei**, who gives her a second chance at life under a new identity: Fugue. Introduced in v2.7 as the Astral Express crew prepares to depart Penacony, Fugue represents both a literal survival and a symbolic fresh start — someone rebuilding an identity and a purpose after the earlier "Tingyun" persona's story came to such a violent end. In-universe voice lines (e.g., from Yukong, who knew the real Tingyun) tie Fugue closely to that earlier character, though the connection is left as strong narrative implication rather than a line of dialogue that flatly states "Fugue is Tingyun."

## Personality

Where "Tingyun" was defined by cheerful helpfulness (in service of a hidden agenda, once Phantylia's deception is accounted for), Fugue's characterization emphasizes breaking enemy toughness and cutting directly through obstacles — a shift in personal philosophy that mirrors her narrative arc from indirection and disguise toward directness and reclaimed agency.

## Related figures

**Ruan Mei** (saved her life), connects directly back to the Tingyun/Phantylia storyline from the Xianzhou Luofu arc.
