# Herta

## Summary

Herta is the reclusive genius founder-director of Herta Space Station, who interacts with the world almost exclusively through small puppet/doll proxies rather than her true body. The entry's core claims — her founding/directing role, the station's location in the Herta Nebula, the v1.0 prologue crisis involving the Stellaron Hunters' infiltration and an Antimatter Legion attack, and her clinically curious personality — check out against established lore and involve no Aeon/Path or faction misattribution. The entry is accurate but intentionally vague on Herta's precise nature, consistent with her role as a deliberately obscure "observer" figure in the story.

## Overview

The eccentric, reclusive genius who founded and directs Herta Space Station — one of the series' most consistently strange characters, in that she is rarely seen in person at all.

## History

Herta built and runs the Space Station that bears her name, a research facility anchored in the Herta Nebula and built around a Space Anchor for studying Stellaron-related phenomena. During the [Kafka](kafka.md)/[Silver Wolf](silver-wolf.md) infiltration and Antimatter Legion attack of the v1.0 prologue (see [`arcs/00-herta-space-station.md`](../../arcs/00-herta-space-station.md)), station leadership (nominally Herta's) coordinates the defense that ultimately protects the Trailblazer and the rest of the station's staff — though Herta herself typically "attends" such crises via a small doll-like proxy rather than her actual body.

## Personality

Brilliant, detached, and openly more interested in interesting problems than in the people attached to them, Herta treats the Trailblazer's Stellaron bonding less as a tragedy and more as a fascinating research opportunity. This clinical curiosity is played partly for comedy and partly as a genuine character trait — she is, by her own apparent standards, a good and fair employer and scientist, just one whose priorities are visibly not primarily human ones.

## Role in the story

Functions as a recurring "observer" figure whose true motives and nature are deliberately kept obscure across the arcs in this compendium. Her Space Station remains a touchstone location the crew returns to periodically, and side content in the v1.4 era ties up loose threads from the original prologue crisis.
