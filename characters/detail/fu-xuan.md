# Fu Xuan

## Summary

Fu Xuan is the Master Diviner and head of the Xianzhou Luofu's Divination Commission, whose "third eye" grants prophetic sight at the cost of chronic pain. Trained by Master Diviner Jingtian on the Xianzhou Yuque, she rejected his fatalism and fled to the Luofu determined to disprove his prediction that she would one day cause his death. That prophecy came full circle during the Third Abundance War, when her Cloudpeer Telescope gambit to save the Xianzhou Fanghu required Jingtian himself to carry it out — and he died doing so, leaving her anti-fatalist philosophy more complicated than simply "vindicated."

## Overview

Master Diviner and head of the Xianzhou Luofu's Divination Commission — a woman who rejected her mentor's fatalism and has spent her career trying to prove that fate can be outcalculated.

## History

Fu Xuan received a mystical "third eye" from a blind elder diviner, granting her powerful prophetic ability at the cost of perpetual physical pain. She trained under Master Diviner Jingtian on the Xianzhou Yuque, who took her as his disciple after divining that she would become the next master diviner — and, just as fatefully, that his own life would end at her hands. Rather than resist this, Jingtian accepted it as predetermined and trained her anyway. Fu Xuan came to reject his resigned, fatalistic worldview — his belief that predetermined destiny simply had to be accepted rather than fought — and resolved to prove with her own hands that his prediction was nothing but delusion. She fled to the Luofu's [Divination Commission](../../factions/detail/divination-commission.md) determined to prove the opposite: that superior calculation and sheer willpower could actually change outcomes fate seemed to have fixed.

This philosophy was put to the test during the Third Abundance War, when the Denizens of Abundance landed large numbers of troops on the Xianzhou Fanghu. Fu Xuan predicted that an offensive stance would lead to defeat and that only a defensive strategy could win the day; when her advice was initially ignored, losses mounted. She then proposed an unconventional, high-risk solution: using the Yuque's Cloudpeer Telescope to send an SOS signal that could summon decisive aid. Only Jingtian held the authority and skill to operate the Telescope, forcing Fu Xuan to confront her former master directly. He carried out the plan she devised and died on the front lines doing so — fulfilling his own prophecy of dying by her hands, even as the gambit turned the tide of the battle. The outcome left Fu Xuan's anti-fatalist convictions more complicated than a clean vindication: the future had been changed, but only by the very fate she had spent her life trying to disprove asserting itself regardless.

## Personality

Confident, demanding, and relentlessly hardworking, Fu Xuan embodies a productive contradiction: she trusts her own calculations to identify "the best solution" in almost any situation, yet her entire career is built on actively resisting pure fatalism. Colleagues have mixed feelings about her exacting standards, but few doubt her competence or her genuine investment in the Xianzhou's welfare.
