# Evernight

## Summary

Evernight is an alternate, far more ancient persona of March 7th, revealed during the Amphoreus arc (v3.x) as an anomalous Chrysos Heir who holds the Coreflame of Time — the domain of the Titan Oronyx — despite never having been a "born" Heir. Her existence, paralleled by Dan Heng's own Permansor Terrae transformation, implies March 7th's amnesiac "found on ice" origin is entangled with Amphoreus's recurring cycle in ways that predate the Trailblazer's arrival. This is late-game, spoiler-heavy story content that should be spot-checked against the latest patch/script.

## Overview

An alternate, far more ancient persona of **[March 7th](march-7th.md)**, uncovered during the Amphoreus arc — one of the clearest signs that March 7th's amnesiac backstory is connected to forces well beyond her apparent age.

## History

Evernight surfaces when March 7th's connection to Amphoreus's cycle is revealed: despite never having been a "born" Chrysos Heir, Evernight claims the Coreflame of Time from the Titan **Oronyx**, gaining command over past, present, and future in a manner that parallels (and complicates) the established rules of Chrysos Heir succession. Her existence strongly implies that March 7th's amnesia and her true origin are tied to Amphoreus's recurring cycle in ways that predate the Trailblazer's arrival by a significant margin.

## Significance

Evernight's emergence, alongside **[Dan Heng](dan-heng.md), Permansor Terrae**'s parallel transformation, suggests that at least two members of the Astral Express's founding crew carry connections to Amphoreus that go back further than anyone previously suspected — recontextualizing March 7th's entire "found on ice, no memory" origin established back in the v1.0 prologue.

## Related figures

**March 7th** (the persona Evernight emerges from), **Oronyx** (the Titan whose Coreflame she claims).
