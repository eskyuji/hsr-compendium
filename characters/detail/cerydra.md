# Cerydra

## Summary

Cerydra, Imperator of the Northern Empire and Chrysos Heir wielding Talanton's Coreflame of Law, is a harsh, drastic ruler whose willingness to make unpopular calls becomes the Amphoreus arc's turning point. At the climax, she lets herself be killed by Hysilens under the Chrysos Heirs' law-rewriting death mechanic, and that death grants the Trailblazer's party administrative access to Scepter δ-me13, rolling back Irontomb's progress and enabling its defeat. Cross-checked against this compendium's other Amphoreus entries (Northern Empire, Irontomb, the Twelve Titans, Chrysos Heirs), the document's core claims are internally consistent and accurate; the main gap was omitting the specific mechanism (Hysilens's role) behind the sacrifice, which has been added.

## Overview

Former Imperator of the Northern Empire and wielder of the Coreflame of Law — a harsh, drastic leader whose final act is an outright sacrifice that saves Amphoreus.

## History

Cerydra ruled the **[Northern Empire](../../factions/detail/northern-empire.md)** as its Imperator, commanding **[Hysilens](hysilens.md)**'s forces and holding the Coreflame of **Talanton**, the Titan of Law, who originally created the Balance Coins currency system underpinning Amphoreus's economy. Her methods as a ruler are consistently described as harsh and willing to embrace drastic measures — a governing style that puts her at odds with softer leaders like **[Aglaea](aglaea.md)**, but which ultimately proves crucial to the arc's resolution.

At the climax of the Amphoreus arc, during the 33,550,337th recurrence of the loop, Cerydra allows herself to be slain by Hysilens rather than fight her off — invoking the Chrysos Heirs' law-rewriting death mechanic, which lets the victor rewrite a law of nature. Her death ultimately grants the Trailblazer's party administrative access to Scepter δ-me13 itself, rolling back **[Irontomb](irontomb.md)**'s progress within the system at the critical moment. This sacrifice is the turning point that finally makes defeating Irontomb possible.

## Personality

Uncompromising and pragmatic to the point of ruthlessness during her reign, Cerydra's final choice recontextualizes her entire arc — someone whose willingness to make hard, unpopular calls turns out to be exactly what the moment of true crisis requires.
