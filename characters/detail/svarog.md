# Svarog

## Summary

Svarog is an ancient guardian automaton originally built to protect Jarilo-VI's Underground mining district; he found and raised the orphaned Clara, becoming her father figure and grounding her worldview in his "infallible" logic. During the Jarilo-VI arc, the Trailblazer's crew helps him learn that unpredictable human qualities — emotion, choice, connection — have real value alongside pure calculation, and his bond with Clara later factors into Belobog's ability to stand on its own against outside pressure (including the IPC). He carries no Aeon or Path allegiance of his own; his role is defined entirely by his devotion to Clara.

## Overview

An ancient guardian robot, originally built to protect Jarilo-VI's mining district, whose rigid commitment to logic and calculation is gradually softened by his bond with the young girl he raised.

## History

Svarog found and effectively raised **[Clara](clara.md)** after she was orphaned in Jarilo-VI's Underground; he became her primary caregiver and, functionally, her father figure. For most of Clara's life, his "infallible" calculations formed the bedrock of her entire worldview. During the Jarilo-VI arc, the Trailblazer's crew works to earn Svarog's trust, ultimately convincing him that unpredictable variables — people, emotion, choice — have genuine value that pure logic can't capture or replace.

This culminates in a striking moment in the arc: Svarog comes to recognize that Clara's capacity for sincere, passionate emotion, not cold logic alone, has real value in the fight to protect Belobog — a conclusion that validates precisely the kind of unpredictability he'd spent his existence trying to control for. In the arc's aftermath, Svarog and Clara's combined strength becomes a symbol of Belobog's independent technological power, later factoring into the city's ability to push back against IPC debt claims.

## Personality

Analytical, protective, and slow to change his mind, Svarog nonetheless proves capable of real growth — his arc is fundamentally about a being built entirely around logical certainty learning to make room for the illogical, irreplaceable value of the person he loves.

## Related figures

**Clara** (ward and partner); indirectly involved in Belobog's later dealings with **[Topaz](topaz.md)** and the [IPC](../../factions/interastral-peace-corporation.md).
