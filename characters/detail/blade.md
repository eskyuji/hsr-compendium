# Blade (Yingxing)

## Summary

Blade (born Yingxing) is a Stellaron Hunter defined by forced near-immortality and a deliberately erased identity. Once a swordsman-in-training on the Xianzhou Luofu and, by most accounts, the close companion of the legendary Cloud Knight swordmaster Jingliu, he lost successive "lives" and pieces of his selfhood through repeated deaths and revivals, and now moves through the galaxy as a near-unkillable blade with nothing left to lose. Kafka's supposed rescue of him is the pretext that redirects the Astral Express to the Xianzhou Luofu in the v1.2 arc, and his duel with Dan Heng there is the direct trigger for Dan Heng's transformation into Imbibitor Lunae.

## Overview

A near-unkillable, self-destructive swordsman and Stellaron Hunter whose true name and history are buried beneath layers of trauma, transformation, and self-erasure.

## History

Blade's birth name is unknown even to him by the time the story picks up — he goes by "Blade" because his original identity, Yingxing, was something he deliberately let go of. Yingxing traveled to Xianzhou territory aboard a merchant ship and became obsessed with forging, a craft that consumed him; the story treats his "first death" and the loss of his birth name as connected events from this era, after which he accepted "only being a weapon and nothing more" as his defining self-concept. He is described, in his own words, as having effectively "traded his body for a sword."

His rescue (or supposed rescue) by **[Kafka](kafka.md)** is the pretext that redirects the Astral Express to the Xianzhou Luofu at the start of the v1.2 arc, and his direct confrontation with **[Dan Heng](dan-heng.md)** is the trigger for Dan Heng's transformation into Imbibitor Lunae — implying Blade's own history is entangled with the Xianzhou's deeper past. In particular, Blade's history is tied to the legendary Cloud Knight swordmaster Jingliu: he is generally understood to have been a swordsman within her circle and her close companion before her mara-struck rampage against her own Cloud Knights, a trauma bound up with Blade's own repeated deaths and his choice to erase his identity. The full extent of this connection is revealed gradually across later content and is not entirely spelled out within the arcs covered by this compendium.

## Personality

Taciturn, self-punishing, and seemingly indifferent to his own survival — Blade's near-immortality reads less like a gift than a curse he can't escape, and his interactions with others carry the weight of someone who has stopped expecting good outcomes for himself.
