# Sunday

## Summary

Sunday is the head of Penacony's Oak Family and public face of the Charmony Festival, whose genuine compassion for those crushed by the Family's exploitation curls into a plot to seize the Dreamscape and forcibly remake reality into one without suffering, choice, or individuality. He frames the scheme as fulfilling the Aeon Ena's principle of Order, but his true endpoint goes further, a world stripped of Aeons, Paths, and free will altogether. Defeated and taken into custody by the Family after his nested-dream gambit fails, he ultimately steps down as head of the Oak Family and departs Penacony aboard the Astral Express to pursue his own "promised land." He is one of the series' most sympathetically written antagonists, his catastrophe born from real compassion pushed past its breaking point.

## Overview

The Oak Family's charismatic representative for the Charmony Festival, whose idealism curdles into an attempt to remake Penacony's entire Dreamscape — one of the series' most sympathetically written antagonists.

## History

Sunday grew up on Penacony alongside his sister **[Robin](robin.md)**, bonded by music and shared dreams; the two once cared for a small bird together as children, a quiet symbol of the freedom and growth they both hoped for. Their mother has passed away, and Sunday still tends her grave with flowers. He showed prodigious musical talent from a young age, able to master piano pieces after hearing them just once.

As head of the [Oak Family](../../factions/penacony-family.md), Sunday inherited real institutional power along with real institutional constraints — by his own account, there are "many things I can't say and many actions I can't take" in his position, even as he privately despises the corruption and exploitation he sees around him. This tension comes to a head during the Charmony Festival, when he's revealed to be secretly conspiring with the Watchmaker, **[Gopher Wood](gopher-wood.md)**, to seize control of Penacony's Dreamscape and remake it into a world "free of suffering" — a goal he frames as honoring the Aeon Ena (Order), but which in truth aims much further: a reality without Aeons, Paths, or free will altogether. He traps the crew in a nested dream of his own making even after their apparent first victory over him, before being defeated for real and taken into custody by the Family.

By v2.3, having faced the consequences of his scheme, Sunday steps back from leading the Oak Family entirely, choosing instead to leave Penacony aboard the Astral Express (with the Family's permission) in pursuit of what he calls his personal "promised land" — a life built around his own convictions rather than institutional duty.

## Personality

Caring, idealistic, and quietly tormented by the gap between what he believes is right and what his position allows him to actually do. His plan to eliminate suffering by eliminating choice is less a villainous scheme than a logical, if catastrophic, extrapolation of real compassion pushed past its breaking point.

## Related figures

**Robin** (sister), **Gopher Wood** (co-conspirator), **[Fugue](fugue.md)/Tingyun** (saved by Ruan Mei after Sunday's era, tangentially connected through the wider cast).
