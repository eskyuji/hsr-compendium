# Honkai: Star Rail — V1.X Lore Compendium: Overview

## Summary

Covers v1.0–1.6, the game's launch arc: three back-to-back Stellaron crises (Herta Space Station, Jarilo-VI/Belobog, Xianzhou Luofu) that introduce the Astral Express crew, the Stellaron Hunters, and the setting's core cosmology of Aeons and Paths.

This compendium covers the story, characters, and factions introduced across Honkai: Star Rail's version 1.0 through 1.6 patches — the game's first full story arc, spanning the launch (April 2023) through the eve of the Penacony arc (v2.0). It does not yet cover Penacony or later content.

## The throughline: Stellarons and Aeons

The overarching plot of v1.x is driven by two pieces of setting cosmology:

**Aeons** are god-like beings, each embodying an abstract concept ("Path") such as Destruction, Preservation, the Hunt, Abundance, Erudition, or Elation. Mortals who commit fully to an Aeon's Path can become that Aeon's **Emanator**, gaining a fraction of its power. The Trailblazer travels aboard the Astral Express partly in search of the Aeon of Trailblaze, whose Path remains mysterious throughout v1.x.

**Stellarons** are crystallized fragments of catastrophe. Across v1.x they read as [Nanook](characters/detail/aeons-reference.md)'s signature — spread by his agents, the **Lord Ravagers** (including [Phantylia](characters/detail/tingyun-phantylia.md), secretly behind the Xianzhou Luofu crisis) — and that's the natural read for a first pass through this era. A later revelation (v3.8, outside this document's scope; see the v3.x compendium) recontextualizes their true origin as Xipe, the Aeon of Harmony, with Destruction's agents exploiting each crisis rather than causing it. The **Stellaron Hunters** ([Kafka](characters/detail/kafka.md), [Silver Wolf](characters/detail/silver-wolf.md), and their founder [Elio](characters/detail/elio.md)) are frequently mistaken for Destruction's agents because their operations keep circling the same crises — but they're tied to the Path of Finality, not to Nanook, and per Elio's Script are ultimately working to oppose Destruction rather than serve it (see [`factions/stellaron-hunters.md`](factions/stellaron-hunters.md)). The v1.x story arc is structured as three back-to-back Stellaron crises: one on Herta Space Station (implanted directly in the Trailblazer, by Kafka), one on Jarilo-VI (the "Eternal Freeze," wielded by Supreme Guardian [Cocolia](characters/detail/cocolia.md)), and one on the Xianzhou Luofu (smuggled into the sacred Ambrosial Arbor by a cult, and secretly detonated by the Ravager Phantylia).

Dates are approximate patch windows and provided for orientation rather than as verified exact release dates.

## Key throughline characters

The **[Trailblazer](characters/detail/trailblazer.md)** (player character), **[March 7th](characters/detail/march-7th.md)**, **[Dan Heng](characters/detail/dan-heng.md)**, **[Himeko](characters/detail/himeko.md)**, and **[Welt Yang](characters/detail/welt-yang.md)** form the core of the Astral Express and are present across all three v1.x arcs. **[Kafka](characters/detail/kafka.md)** and **[Silver Wolf](characters/detail/silver-wolf.md)** of the Stellaron Hunters recur as antagonist-adjacent figures who manipulate events from the shadows in every arc — their true allegiance (Path of Finality, ultimately opposed to Destruction) only becomes clear much later in the story. See [`factions/astral-express.md`](factions/astral-express.md) and [`factions/stellaron-hunters.md`](factions/stellaron-hunters.md) for details.
